Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Fish Consumption Guidelines

No fish consumption guidelines are available for this lake. For more information, see the "Fish Consumption Advice" pages at the Minnesota Department of Health.


Status of the Fishery (as of 06/17/2004)

A resurvey was conducted on Ringo Lake during mid June of 2004. Ringo is a moderate sized (716 acres), shallow (10 feet maximum depth), aerated, and productive lake located in Kandiyohi County. Water clarity was poor (secchi=1.17 feet) and brown stained in June of 2004, but typical for Ringo Lake. Nutrient levels were high (total phosphorus=0.118 ppm), but also typical for Ringo Lake. Blue-green algae blooms are common during the late summer months on Ringo Lake. Sago pondweed was the most frequently occurring submergent vegetation species in Ringo Lake. Submergent vegetation densities were generally low throughout the lake. Emergent vegetation (hardstem bulrush, cattails) was abundant throughout the lake, especially near the islands, bay areas, and peninsulas. Shoalwater substrates consist primarily of sand, detritus, silt, and gravel. A public shore fishing area is located along the north shore of Ringo. A public access boat ramp is located along the northwest corner. The immediate watershed consists primarily of wetlands, hardwoods, agricultural row crops, gravel pits, asphalt plant, and residential housing. Presently, there are 14 homes located along the Ringo lakeshore. Several new homes were constructed along the northwest corner within the past ten years. A new housing development is occurring along the southwest side of the lake. There are six small inlets that enter Ringo Lake. The outlet connects to Long Lake. The DNR Spicer Fisheries Office manages Ringo primarily for walleye, northern pike, and black crappie. In recent years, walleye fry have been stocked into Ringo Lake annually because walleye eggs have been for statewide hatching operations. If a fry stocking is a failure, then the lake will generally be stocked again with fry the following spring. Walleye fingerling stocking is contingent in Ringo after successive fry stocking failures.

Bluegill numbers were moderate in 2004 (3.00 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The bluegill historical average catch rate is 1.58 fish/trapnet for Ringo. The 2004 bluegill average size was moderate (0.18 pounds and 5.72 inches) from trapnets. The bluegill historical average weight is 0.16 pounds from trapnets. The largest bluegill captured was 7.36 inches in the 2004 resurvey. Bluegill growth rates for Ringo were above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-4. The 2001 bluegill year class comprised 46% of the 2004 bluegill resurvey catch.

Black crappie numbers were abundant in 2004 (35.89 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The black crappie historical average catch rate is 21.40 fish/trapnet for Ringo Lake. The 2004 black crappie average size was small (0.15 pounds and 6.09 inches) from trapnets. The black crappie historical average weight is 0.22 pounds from trapnets. The largest black crappie captured was 12.91 inches in the 2004 resurvey. The 2004 catch rate of black crappie 8.00 inches and larger was moderate (6.83 fish/trapnet) in Ringo. Black crappie growth rates in Ringo were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-4. The 2003 black crappie year class comprised 52% of the 2004 black crappie resurvey catch.

Northern pike numbers were moderate in 2004 (3.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The northern pike historical average catch rate is 3.45 fish/gillnet for Ringo. The 2004 northern pike average size was moderate to small (2.37 pounds and 23.60 inches) from gillnets. The northern pike historical average weight is 3.19 pounds from gillnets. Northern pike growth rates in Ringo were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-3. The 2001 northern pike year class comprised 87% of the 2004 resurvey northern pike catch.

Walleye numbers were moderate in 2004 (7.33 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, but low compared to the walleye historical average catch rate (22.84 fish/gillnet) for Ringo. The 2004 walleye average size was moderate (1.83 pounds and 15.98 inches) from gillnets. The walleye historical average weight is 1.03 pounds from gillnets. The largest walleye captured was 26.97 inches in the 2004 resurvey. The catch rate of walleye 15.00 inches and larger walleye was moderate (3.33 fish/gillnet) in 2004. Walleye growth rates in Ringo were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-3, and above the normal ranges for ages 4-8. The 2001 walleye year class comprised 26% of the 2004 resurvey walleye catch. Walleye natural reproduction in Ringo is frequent, but variable for numbers produced.

A fall electrofishing survey of Ringo Lake was conducted on September 27, 2004. The fall electrofishing survey was conducted to evaluate a 2004 spring walleye fry stocking (441,943 fry). There were moderate young of year "YOY" walleye numbers (64.00 YOY/hour, 5.16 inches average length) captured in 2004. Other area walleye fry stocked lakes had generally low YOY walleye catch rates in 2004 fall electrofishing surveys. The Ringo Lake fall electrofishing historical average catch rate and length are 104.72 YOY/hour and 5.81 inches respectively. Walleye fry were also stocked into Ringo Lake during 1994, 1998, 2000, 2002, and 2003. The highest fall electrofishing catch rate of YOY walleye occurred in 1994 (520.00 YOY/hour, 4.47 inches average length) for a fry stocked year. The highest fall electrofishing catch rate for YOY walleye due to natural reproduction occurred in 1995 (129.00 fish/hour, 6.16 inches average length).

Yellow perch numbers were low in 2004 (0.33 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The yellow perch historical average catch rate is 1.63 fish/gillnet for Ringo. The 2004 yellow perch average weight and length were 0.07 pounds and 5.59 inches from gillnets. The yellow perch historical average weight is 0.13 pounds from gillnets.

Black bullhead numbers were moderate to abundant in 2004 (65.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The black bullhead historical average catch rate is 44.53 fish/gillnet for Ringo. The 2004 black bullhead average size was moderate (0.26 pounds and 7.86 inches) from gillnets. The black bullhead historical average weight is 0.29 pounds from gillnets.

Carp numbers were moderate in 2004 (1.33 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, but below the carp historical average catch rate (4.51 fish/gillnet) for Ringo Lake. The 2004 carp average size was moderate (4.26 pounds and 21.53 inches) from gillnets. The carp historical average weight is 2.91 pounds from gillnets.

Current fish management activities on Ringo include monitoring the fish population on a periodic basis, monitoring winter dissolved oxygen levels, assisting the county with the operation of the aeration system, protecting aquatic vegetation through the permit process, participating in local watershed initiatives, and stocking various fish species as warranted. The Ringo fishery will be sampled in the 2008 summer for all fish species.
