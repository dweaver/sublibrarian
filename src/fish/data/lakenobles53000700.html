* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Fish Consumption Guidelines

Status of the Fishery (as of 06/01/2004)

Indian Lake, in Nobles County, is a 204 acre lake that is managed primarily for both black and white crappie. It is managed secondarily for walleye and northern pike. This class 43 lake has a history of low dissolved oxygen and thus has been opened to liberalized fishing in the past. Historical stocking has consisted primarily of walleye fry and fingerlings. Black crappie were stocked in 1979, 1983, and 1990 whereas yellow perch were stocked once in 1981. Despite the fact that northern pike have only been stocked once in 14 years, the population seems to be somewhat self-sufficient. The operational plan calls for stocking walleye fingerlings every third year. Walleye fingerlings were stocked in the fall of 2000 (204 lbs) and 2003 (180 lbs) and adults in 2003 (60 lbs). A survey was conducted during the week of June 1, 2004 using 3 gill nets and 9 trap nets.

The black crappie catch rate was less than half that of the 2000 survey. Despite the fact that the numbers are down, the sizes of fish are quite nice. Total lengths ranged from 7.4 to 12.6 inches and averaged just over 10 inches. This population had the largest black crappie sizes of all surveys conducted in 2004. They were in excellent shape and quite plump. Growth was above average as an Age 2 black crappie averaged nearly 8 inches. The take home message is that if you are wanting to find a lake to fish black crappie, Indian Lake has some real "slabs" that could provide phenomenal fishing.

As with black crappies, the white crappie catch rate was down from the 2000 survey, but was still excellent compared to similar lakes in the Windom Area. Often when you see a decline in numbers, the trade-off is an increase in the sizes of fish caught. This is the same scenario as the black crappie in that the numbers declined since the last survey but the sizes of fish are excellent. Total lengths of white crappie ranged from 8.1 to 12.6 and averaged 10.5 inches, and condition of fish was above average. Growth was also above average as an Age 2 white crappie averaged over 8.4 inches. Similar to the black crappie population, some phenomenal white crappie fishing can be had if you want some real "slabs".

The catch rate for walleye was the best ever recorded during surveys on Indian Lake. Total lengths ranged from 12.6 to 22.5 inches and averaged 14.7 inches. The fish were quite healthy, being plump and in good condition. Growth of walleye was above average for the Windom Area as an Age 2 walleye was nearly 13.5 inches. Most of the walleye sampled corresponded to the fingerling stockings in 2000 and 2003. No doubt about it that these fish would be worth the time for you walleye anglers. This lake would be an excellent lake to fish crappies during the day and target the excellent walleye population at dusk.

The northern pike catch rate was average for this type of lake. Northern pike have only been stocked once (February 2001) in the last 14 years. Varying northern pike numbers have been present in all surveys since the initial survey conducted in 1987. Total lengths ranged from 17.3 to 27.4 inches and averaged 20.6 inches. Growth of northern pike was average for the Windom Area as an Age 2 northern averaged 18.8 inches while an Age 3 was nearly 21 inches. It might be worth your time to try some "tip-up" fishing for some northerns during the winter.

The yellow perch catch rate was similar to that in the 2000 survey. Total lengths ranged from 6.9 to 11.1 inches and averaged 9.2 inches. Yellow perch have never been stocked into Indian Lake but appear to be self-sustaining. The yellow perch are quite plump and in good condition.

A total of 12 species of fish were sampled during the survey of 2004, including bigmouth buffalo, black bullhead, black crappie, bluegill, common carp, freshwater drum, northern pike, walleye, white crappie, white sucker, yellow bullhead, and yellow perch.

To promote and maintain healthy fish populations, pollution and other inputs need to be controlled. Fish habitats are directly affected by water quality. Nutrient, sediment and other waste inputs can drastically alter the biological, chemical, and physical components of a lake. It is paramount to maintain if not improve the current water quality of Indian Lake through watershed management to preserve this fishery for future generations to enjoy.
