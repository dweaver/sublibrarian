Status of the Fishery (as of 08/21/2000)

Section 12 Lake is a 156 acre bass and panfish lake southeast of Aitkin. The lake has a maximum depth of 40 feet and 57 percent of the lake is less than 15 feet deep. There is a state owned public access on the northwest corner of the lake. Heavy mats of northern water milfoil may be found in shallow areas, especially on the south and west shorelines making boating difficult. The fish community sampled in Section 12 Lake included black crappie, bluegill, largemouth bass, northern pike, walleye, and yellow perch. Black crappie were caught in abundance that was above normal for this lake type, however most of the fish sampled were small, 3 to 5 year old fish, with the average size just under 7.5 inches. Black crappie fishing should be good in a couple years when these fish attain desirable size. Anglers can expect average fishing for other panfish species with bluegills and pumpkinseed sunfish available in abundance that is normal for this type of lake. However, bluegills tend to be slow growing and small, having an average size of less than 5.5 inches. There is a strong year-class of largemouth bass from 1997, which should provide some fun angling opportunities as they attain lengths over 15 inches. The presence of big largemouth bass greater than 17 inches, documented in this assessment and a previous electrofishing assessment, is a good sign and suggests low mortality. Release of large bass will continue to maintain the health of this fishery in the future. The northern pike fishery has remained stable in abundance within the lower end of the normal range for this lake type, which is good considering the common trend toward overabundance observed in many surrounding lakes. The presence of several larger northern pike, greater than 27 inches, likewise indicates a more balanced, lightly exploited fishery. Release of northern pike larger than 22 inches in favor of harvest of smaller fish will help to maintain the quality of this fishery. In addition to northern pike, large walleye are occasionally caught. One eighteen-year-old, 22 inch walleye was netted in the 2000 assessment.
