Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Fish Consumption Guidelines

Status of the Fishery (as of 07/23/2007)

East Goose, near the town of Lynd, is a 139-acre lake located in Lyon County. East Goose has a maximum depth of 9 feet and has had an aeration system since 1986. It has not had a significant history of winterkills, as it has been opened to liberalized fishing only 3 times from 1952 to 1986. Because Goose Lake is relatively close to Marshall, this small lake has a history of heavy angling pressure (up to 3 boats per acre). One of the long-range goals was to produce more consistent year classes of fish to "spread" out the angling and harvest pressure. This goal resulted in a more frequent, but lower density, fry stocking regime. Goose is managed primarily for walleye and secondarily for yellow perch. A population assessment was conducted during the week of July 23, 2007 using 2 gill nets and 9 trap nets.

Despite a pocket winterkill during the winter of 2006-2007, the abundance of walleye was impressive in the survey assessment in 2007. Catch rates have been higher only once (1999) based on the historical survey data. Total lengths ranged from 7 to 21 inches with an average of just short of 15 inches. Although the "plumpness" of fish could have been better, growth rates of walleye were excellent as an average length Age 2 walleye was 15.5 inches and an Age 3 was 17.3 inches. In the last 5-10 years the Windom office has been generally reluctant to open a lake to liberalized fishing because a "complete" winterkill is rare. This small lake is definitely a diamond in the rough and should be considered for fishing walleyes.

Yellow perch numbers were within the expected range. The catch rate was 12.5/gill net set and over the years has varied from 3.3 to 93.0/set. Total lengths ranged from 6.1 to 12.4 inches with an average of 8.8 inches. Although their abundance has been higher, decent numbers of "keepers" could be caught. If the perch are not biting, target the black crappie.

The black crappie catch rate has never been higher. Total lengths ranged from 4.3 to 13.2 inches with a mean of 6.0 inches. Despite the abundance of smaller fish, targeting black crappies could provide some good action. In a few years, there could be some good "keepers".

Black bullhead abundance was within the expected range. The black bullhead catch rate was 62/gill net set and historically has varied from 35 to 162.0/gill net set.

Despite a pocket winterkill, common carp abundance was higher than the normal range (1.0 to 13.8/set). Total lengths ranged from 8.7 to 25.6 inches with a mean of 14.8 inches. Based on this data, this would suggest the winterkill was localized. Other species sampled were largemouth bass and orangespotted sunfish.

Efforts to improve the health of the lake should focus on best management practices within the watershed. Improvements and education in land stewardship will often have secondary benefits to the lake in the way of quality and quantity of habitat. Generally, improvements in the quality and quantity of lake habitat will mean direct improvements of the fishery.
