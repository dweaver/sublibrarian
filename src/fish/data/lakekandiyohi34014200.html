Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 06/26/2008)

A population assessment was conducted on George Lake in late June of 2008. George is a small (231 acres), moderately deep (34 feet maximum), and low to moderately productive lake located in Kandiyohi County. The water clarity was good on June 30, 2008 (secchi=11.0 feet). Dissolved oxygen levels were above 5.0 ppm down to 24 feet deep. Submergent vegetation (muskgrass, various pondweeds, water moss, coontail, northern milfoil, bladderwort, and filamentous algae) was moderate to abundant within the lake. Emergent vegetation is limited to a few small stands of hardstem bulrush. Shoalwater substrates consist mostly of sand, gravel, and rubble. The immediate watershed is small with hardwoods, wetlands, residential housing, agricultural row crops, and grasslands. There are three small inlets that flow into George Lake. The DNR Spicer Fisheries Office manages George primarily for largemouth bass, walleye, bluegill and secondarily for black crappie, yellow perch, and northern pike. George Lake was a study lake for a DNR Research project on walleye stocking.

Black crappie were not captured in the 2008 trapnets or gillnets. The black crappie historical average catch rates are 1.27 fish/trapnet and 6.17 fish/gillnet. The black crappie historical average weight is approximately 0.27 pounds from both gillnets and trapnets. Black crappie adults (1,077 fish, 336 pounds) were stocked into George during 2000.

Northern pike numbers were abundant in 2008 (11.20 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for George Lake (25th=1.20, 50th=3.92, 75th=5.35 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The northern pike historical average catch rate is 4.08 fish/gillnet. The 2008 northern pike average size was small (2.50 pounds and 21.59 inches) from gillnets. The northern pike historical average weight is 2.91 pounds from gillnets. The 2008 catch rate of quality size (21.00 inches) and larger northern pike was moderate (4.80 fish/gillnet). The 2008 catch rate of preferred size (28.00 inches) and larger northern pike was low to moderate (1.40 fish/gillnet). Northern pike growth rates in George were below the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-4 and within the normal ranges for ages 5-7. The 2004 northern pike year class comprised 37% of the 2008 northern pike population assessment catch.

Yellow perch numbers were low in 2008 (0.40 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for George Lake (25th=2.65, 50th=5.50, 75th=10.50 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The yellow perch historical average catch rate is 6.76 fish/gillnet. The 2008 yellow perch average size was moderate (0.17 pounds and 6.99 inches) from gillnets. The yellow perch historical average weight is 0.14 pounds from gillnets.

Walleye numbers were low in 2008 (0.60 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for George Lake (25th=1.70, 50th=5.60, 75th=6.80 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The walleye historical catch rate is 5.19 fish/gillnet for George. Low numbers of small walleye (7.23 inches average length, 2007 year class) were captured in the 2008 trapnets (0.50 fish/trapnet). The 2008 walleye average size was large (3.38 pounds and 20.13 inches) from gillnets. The walleye historical average weight was 1.55 pounds from gillnets. Walleye growth rates were generally above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-6.

A 2006 spring electrofishing survey was conducted to evaluate a 2005 walleye fryling stocking for a DNR Research study. Walleye fry were marked with oxytetracycline, then stocked as fingerlings in 2001 (112 pounds, 1,792 fish), fry in 2003 (118,731 fish), and frylings in 2005 (17,593). No walleye were stocked in 2002 or 2004. There were no walleye captured from the 2005 year class in the 2006 spring electrofishing survey. Unmarked walleye fingerlings (4,932 fish, 240 pounds) were stocked into George during the 2007 fall.

Largemouth bass were abundant (90.00 fish/hour) in the 2008 spring electrofishing survey compared to the Spicer Area average catch rate (47.78 fish/hour). The largemouth bass historical average catch rate is 66.17 fish/hour for George Lake. The 2008 largemouth bass average size was small (0.70 pounds and 10.60 inches) from spring electrofishing. The largemouth bass historical average weight and length are 0.69 pounds and 10.51 inches from spring electrofishing for George Lake. The 2008 catch rate of quality size (12.00 inches) and larger largemouth bass was moderate (13.95 fish/hour). The 2008 catch rate of preferred size (15.00 inches) and larger largemouth bass was low (1.40 fish/hour). Largemouth bass growth rates were below the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-8. The 2003 largemouth bass year class accounted for 43% of the 2008 spring electrofishing catch.

Bluegill numbers were moderate in 2008 (31.40 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal ranges for George Lake (25th=27.80, 50th=39.72, 75th=73.21 fish/trapnet) and similar lakes. The bluegill historical average catch rate is 55.02 fish/trapnet for George Lake. The 2008 bluegill gillnet catch rate (11.80 fish/gillnet) was slightly above the George Lake historical average (9.24 fish/gillnet). The 2008 bluegill average size was small (0.13 pounds and 5.14 inches) from trapnets, but moderate (0.22 pounds and 6.47 inches) from gillnets. The bluegill historical average weights are 0.15 and 0.12 pounds from gillnets and trapnets respectively. The catch rate of quality size (6.00 inches) and larger bluegill was moderate (11.44 fish/trapnet) in 2008. Bluegill growth rates in George Lake were below the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-5, but within the normal ranges for ages 6-8. The 2002 year class comprised 25% of the 2008 bluegill population assessment catch. The 2003 and 2005 year classes each comprised 21% of the 2008 bluegill catch.

Pumpkinseed sunfish numbers were low to moderate in 2008 (2.60 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal ranges for George (25th=2.28, 50th=3.53, 75th=5.03 fish/trapnet) and similar lakes. The 2008 pumpkinseed sunfish gillnet catch rate was moderate (3.00 fish/gillnet). The historical average pumpkinseed sunfish catch rates are 1.40 fish/gillnet and 4.27 fish/trapnet for George. The 2008 pumpkinseed sunfish average weights were moderate (0.25 from gillnets and 0.19 pounds from trapnets).

Black bullhead numbers were moderate in 2008 (2.40 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The black bullhead historical average catch rate is 1.91 fish/gillnet for George. The 2008 black bullhead average size was large (0.96 pounds and 11.91 inches) from gillnets. The black bullhead historical average weight is 0.89 pounds from gillnets.

Yellow bullhead numbers were abundant to moderate in 2008 (4.40 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for George (25th=0.45, 50th=1.63, 75th=4.05 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The yellow bullhead historical average catch rate is 2.37 fish/gillnet. The 2008 yellow bullhead average weight was large (0.81 pounds) from gillnets. The yellow bullhead historical average weight is 0.67 pounds from gillnets.

Current fish management activities on George include monitoring the fish population on a periodic basis, protecting aquatic vegetation through the permit process, participating in local watershed initiatives, and stocking various fish species as warranted. The George Lake fishery is scheduled for sampling in the 2013 spring and summer for all fish species. ?
