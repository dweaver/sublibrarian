Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 07/28/2008)

A resurvey of Green Lake was conducted in late July of 2008. Green is a large (5,406 acres), deep (110 feet maximum), and moderately productive lake located in Kandiyohi County. Water levels were normal during the early summer, but low by the fall of 2008. Water clarity was good (8.0  10.0 feet) for Green Lake in the 2008 summer. Total phosphorus level was low (0.014 ppm) during early June of 2008. Ice out was late in 2008 (April 30th). Dissolved oxygen levels were above 4.0 ppm in depths down to 32 feet during late July of 2008. The shoreline is fully developed with the exception of the outlet area on the east side of the lake. There were 641 homes, 2 county parks, 1 bed & breakfast, 2 resorts (11 cabins), 13 apartment buildings, and 26 homes in a common interest community located within a connected bay (Indian Beach). The immediate watershed is varied with the City of Spicer, residential homes, wetlands, hardwood lots, and agricultural row crops. There are approximately 48 inlets (mostly small tiles) that drain into Green Lake. The Middle Fork of the Crow River (Nest Lake outlet) is the largest inlet that flows into Green Lake along the north shore at the Ye Olde Mill Inn Dam and exits along the east shore of Green Lake. Nutrient runoff is low to moderate primarily from nearby residential and city sources, and upstream sources from the Middle Fork of the Crow River via Nest Lake. Numerous storm water retention ponds were built during the 2003 State Highway 23 Four-Lane Project along the west side of Green Lake. There are six public access sites and one shore fishing site (outlet) on the lake. Green is a popular fishery for walleye, northern pike, smallmouth bass, and bluegill. Green Lake receives high recreational use during the summer months. Green Lake is managed primarily for walleye and secondarily for northern pike, yellow perch, tullibee, bluegill, and smallmouth bass.

Overall aquatic vegetation trends for mean abundance and frequency occurrence were generally greater in 2008 than previous surveys. There were 27 aquatic vegetation species/groups sampled in 2008 compared to 22 in 1994, 31 in 2003, and 21 in 2007. Water level and precipitation conditions were normal during the early summer, but dry conditions were the rule during the late summer of 2008. Water clarity was poor during the late summer of 2003 (4.83 feet) compared to 1994 (7.50 feet), 2007 (7.20 feet) and 2008 (9.0 feet). Aquatic vegetation densities generally increase during low water due to increased water clarity conditions and light penetration to substrate surface areas that were previously inaccessible. The maximum depth of vegetation was the greatest in 2007 (40 feet) compared to 2008 (31 feet), 2003 (30 feet) or 1994 (35 feet). The 2008 aquatic vegetation species trends for frequency occurrence and mean abundance include increases of river pondweed, curly-leaf pondweed, Eurasian milfoil, flat-stem pondweed, Fries pondweed, water celery, stonewort, Robbins pondweed, muskgrass, narrow-leaf pondweed, and bladderwort. Chara (muskgrass) frequency occurrence was slightly higher in 2008 (98%) than 2007 (94%), 2003 (96%) and 1994 (92%). Mean abundance of muskgrass was 49.0% for 2008, 32.1% for 1994, 29.3% for 2003, and 44.3% for 2007. Stonewort (nitella) appreciably increased in 2008 (50% occurrence, 24.3 mean abundance) compared to previous surveys. Vegetation species that were relatively unchanged in frequency occurrence or mean abundance from 2003 to 2007 were curly-leaf pondweed, northern milfoil, and hardstem bulrush. Current notable vegetation species that trends decreased in frequency occurrence and mean abundance included filamentous algae, sago pondweed, water moss, variable-leaf pondweed, hardstem bulrush, clasping-leaf pondweed, leafy pondweed, naiad, and waterlily species.

Low densities of Eurasian water milfoil have been documented in Green Lake primarily near the Spicer public access, swimming beach, city dock, Indian Beach Lagoon, and several isolated areas since 2000. Efforts to chemically eradicate various Eurasian water milfoil stands were overall unsuccessful in 2000, 2001, 2007, and 2008. Eurasian milfoil occurred in 20% (10 transects) of the 2008 transects, 16% (8) of the 2007 transects, and 4%(2) of the 2003 transects. It is not surprising that frequency occurrence has increased probably due to the ease of Eurasian milfoil fragmenting and colonizing new suitable areas. Mean abundance of Eurasian milfoil within the transects increased from 1.3% in 2003, to 5.3% in 2007, and to 6.0% in 2008. It appears that Eurasian milfoil mean abundance has increased at a slower rate from 2007 to 2008 than from 2003 to 2007. Widely scattered Eurasian milfoil stands were observed in off shore "break" areas at a mean depth of 8.6 feet during 2007. Most large stands of Eurasian milfoil on Green Lake have been located near storm water inlets. Watershed projects that may reduce sediment and nutrient inputs from storm water inlets will be important for controlling Eurasian milfoil in these patchy areas. Widespread-extensive matting of Eurasian water milfoil is unlikely to occur through out Green Lake due to frequent windswept conditions, the presence of relatively low nitrogen (ammonium) nutrient levels and unsuitable dominant substrates (boulder, rubble, gravel) present in the majority of shoalwater areas.

Experimental regulations for northern pike, smallmouth bass, and largemouth bass were enacted in Green Lake during 1997 as part of a statewide research study. The goals were to evaluate the success or failure of certain types of experimental regulations, and produce trophy fisheries of bass and northern pike in Green Lake. A public input meeting in regards to the future of Green Lake experimental regulations occurred during the 2005 fall. In December of 2005, the DNR decided to end all experimental regulations on Green Lake (largemouth bass, smallmouth bass and northern pike) and return to statewide regulations on May 13, 2006 for northern pike and May 27, 2006 for bass. This decision was based both on public input and the analyzed data collected from netting and creel surveys.

Bluegill numbers were moderate to abundant in 2008 (47.60/trapnet) compared to the normal ranges for Green Lake (25th=28.04, 50th=47.60, 75th=65.77 fish/trapnet) for Green Lake and similar lakes. The Green Lake bluegill historical average catch rate is 49.28 fish/trapnet. The 2008 bluegill average size was small (0.13 pounds and 5.54 inches) from the trapnets. The bluegill historical average weight is 0.12 pounds from trapnets. The 2008 catch rate of quality size (6.00 inches) and larger bluegill was high (16.80 fish/trapnet) and above the historical average (11.65 fish/trapnet). The 2008 catch rate of preferred size (8.00 inches) and larger bluegill (0.40 fish/trapnet) was low. Bluegill growth rates were below the Spicer Area and Green Lake normal ranges for ages 2-5, but within the normal ranges for ages 1 and 6-8. The 2005 year class comprised 35% of the 2008 total bluegill gillnet and trapnet catch. Low young of year "YOY" bluegill numbers (3.30 fish/seine haul) were captured in the 2008 shoreline seine hauls.

Black crappie numbers were moderate to abundant in 2008 (1.58 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for Green Lake (25th=0.08, 50th=0.42, 75th=1.54 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The Green Lake black crappie historical average catch rate is 0.81 fish/gillnet. The 2008 black crappie average size was moderate (0.60 pounds and 9.50 inches) from gillnets. The black crappie historical average length is 7.88 inches from gillnets. The 2008 catch rate of quality size (8.00 inches) and larger black crappie was moderate (1.17 fish/gillnet) and above the historical average (0.26 fish/gillnet). Black crappie growth rates were between the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-4, and above the normal range for age 5. Growth rates were within the Green Lake normal ranges for ages 1-2 and above the normal ranges for ages 3-6. The 2005 black crappie year class comprised 65% of the 2007 black crappie survey catch and 50% of the 2008 survey catch. No YOY black crappie were captured in the 2008 shoreline seine hauls.

Northern pike numbers were low in 2008 (1.92 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for Green (25th=2.25, 50th=2.75, 75th=3.25 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The Green Lake northern pike historical average catch rate is 2.78 fish/gillnet. The 2008 northern pike average size was moderate (3.65 pounds and 24.92 inches) from gillnets. The northern pike historical average weight and length are 3.09 pounds and 23.13 inches from gillnets. Northern pike captured in 2003-2006 surveys were notably lean in appearance compared to fish captured in the assessments prior to 2003. Large northern pike (>24 inches) condition factors (Fulton K-factor) were near or below the Green normal range (0.591  0.662 K-factor) for 2008 (0.605 K-factor) and every year since 1999 except 2001 and 2002. Low numbers of forage fish (yellow perch and tullibee) were documented in Green Lake surveys from 2003-2008. The 2008 catch rate of preferred size (28.00 inches) and larger northern pike was low to moderate (0.42 fish/gillnet) and near the historical average (0.43 fish/gillnet). Spearing pressure and harvest of large northern pike was high during the 2006-07 winter following the return to statewide regulations. Northern pike growth rates were within or above the Green Lake normal ranges for ages 1-3 and below the 25th quartiles for ages 4-6. Growth rates were generally near or above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-6. The 2004 year class comprised 31% of the 2008 northern pike gillnet and trapnet catch.

Smallmouth bass numbers were moderate in 2008 (1.67 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for Green Lake (25th=0.54, 50th=1.67, 75th=2.58 fish/gillnet) but above the normal range for similar lakes. The smallmouth bass historical average catch rate is 1.97 fish/gillnet for Green Lake. The 2008 smallmouth bass average size was moderate (1.81 pounds and 14.24 inches) from gillnets. The smallmouth bass historical average weight and length are 1.55 pounds and 13.44 inches from gillnets. The 2008 catch rate of preferred size (14.00 inches) and larger smallmouth bass (1.00 fish/gillnet) was similar to the historical average (1.27 fish/gillnet). Several moderate smallmouth year classes (2005, 2001, 2000) comprised the 2008 smallmouth bass gillnet catch. Smallmouth bass growth rates were below the Green Lake normal ranges for ages 2-7. The 2008 smallmouth bass condition factor (Fulton K-Factor) was 1.410. Smallmouth bass condition factors have been near or below the Green normal range since 2000. YOY smallmouth bass numbers was low in 2008 shoreline seine hauls (0.90 fish/seine haul, 1.93 inches average length). Crayfish, an important smallmouth bass forage species, were not captured in 2008 or 2007, low numbers in 2006 (0.1 crayfish/gillnet) and 2005 (0.2 crayfish/gillnet). No crayfish were caught in the 2004, 2003 or 2002 gillnets. The crayfish historical average catch rate is 9.0 crayfish/gillnet for Green.

Tullibee "cisco" numbers were moderate to abundant in the 2008 summer (2.58 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for Green Lake (25th=0.50 50th=1.00, 75th=2.68 fish/gillnet) similar lakes. The tullibee historical average summer catch rate is 2.42 fish/gillnet for Green Lake. All of the tullibee were captured in one gillnet station located along the east portion of the lake located in 30-40 feet deep. The 2008 summer tullibee average size was large (1.42 pounds and 14.35 inches) from gillnets. The tullibee historical average weight is 0.51 pounds from gillnets. Tullibee growth rates were below the Green Lake normal ranges for ages 1-2, within the normal ranges for ages 3-6, and above the normal range for age 7. The 2004 and 2003 year classes each comprised 37% of the 2008 tullibee summer gillnet catch.

Yellow perch numbers were low in 2008 (4.75 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for Green Lake (25th=6.67, 50th=15.80, 75th=23.13 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The yellow perch historical average catch rate is 16.54 fish/gillnet for Green Lake. The 2008 yellow perch average size was small (0.11 pounds and 6.31 inches) from gillnets. The yellow perch historical average weight is 0.13 pounds from gillnets. The 2008 catch rate of quality size (8.00 inches) and larger yellow perch was low (0.25 fish/gillnet). Yellow perch growth rates were generally below both the Spicer Area and Green Lake normal ranges for ages 1-4. The 2005 (52%)and 2004 (36%) yellow perch year classes comprised the majority of the 2008 yellow perch gillnet catch. The 2008 yellow perch condition factor (Fulton K-Factor) was 1.084. Yellow perch condition factors have been near or below the Green normal range since 2003. YOY yellow perch abundance was low (0.20 fish/seine haul, 1.85 inches average length) in the 2008 shoreline seine hauls.

Walleye numbers were moderate in 2008 (8.17 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for Green Lake (25th=7.69, 50th=9.42, 75th=9.96 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The walleye historical average catch rate is 9.12 fish/gillnet for Green Lake. The 2008 walleye average size was moderate (1.56 pounds and 15.35 inches) from gillnets. The walleye historical average weight and length are 1.26 pounds and 14.66 inches from gillnets. The 2008 catch rate of quality size (15.00 inches) and larger walleye (3.92 fish/gillnet) was near the historical average (3.87 fish/gillnet) for Green Lake. An unexpected number of older walleye from the 1994, 1995, 2000, and 2001 year classes were captured in the 2008 gillnets and trapnets. The walleye year classes of 2006 (30%), 2005 (23%), and 2004 (19%) comprised the majority of the 2008 gillnet catch. The 2004 year class (stocked and natural reproduction) comprised 41% of the total 2007 walleye assessment gillnet catch. The recent walleye year classes since 2001 are probably due mostly to stocking. Walleye growth rates were generally below the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-3 and within the normal ranges for ages 4-8. However, growth rates were near or above the Green Lake normal ranges for ages 1-9. The 2008 walleye condition factor (Fulton K-Factor) was 0.932. Walleye condition factors have been above the Green Lake normal range since 1999.

A fall electrofishing survey of Green Lake was conducted during early October of 2008. The 2008 fall electrofishing survey was conducted to evaluate walleye natural reproduction and a 2008 walleye fry stocking in Green Lake. Oxytetracycline "OTC" marked walleye fry (approximately 1.03 million/year) were stocked during 2007 and 2008. The walleye fry were stocked as a 10% return of walleye eggs taken for the statewide propagation program. There were low young of year "YOY" walleye numbers (7.00 YOY/hour, 5.97 inches average length) captured in 2008. Marked fish from 2007 and 2008 fry stockings accounted for 43% of YOY walleye catch and 33% of the yearling walleye catch in the 2008 fall electrofishing. Marked yearling walleye accounted for 29% of the 2008 summer yearling walleye gillnet catch. A marked three-year old walleye from a small stocking (666 frylings) was also captured in the 2008 summer gillnets. The Green Lake fall electrofishing historical average catch rate and length are 14.29 YOY/hour and 6.79 inches. The Spicer Area YOY walleye historical average catch rate is 76.32 YOY/hour from lakes fry stocked and lakes with natural reproduction. The highest fall electrofishing catch rate of YOY walleye for Green occurred in 1994 (54.00 YOY/hour, 6.15 inches average size). Walleye fingerlings (51,641 fish, 3,105 pounds), yearlings (5,016 fish, 209 pounds), and fry (1,028,856 fish) were stocked into Green during early April-November of 2008. There were 30,787 marked OTC stocked in Green during the fall of 2008. Green Lake has been stocked annually with walleye fry from 2000-2008 except for 2002. Various combinations of walleye fingerlings, yearlings, or adults have been stocked yearly (2,000  4,000 pounds/year) since 2000 into Green Lake. During the 1990s, walleye fry were stocked in 1996 and fingerlings/yearlings/adults were stocked in 1994, 1992, 1990.

Rock bass numbers were abundant in 2008 (13.83 fish/gillnet) and the highest recorded value for Green Lake. The 2008 rock bass abundance was above the normal ranges for Green Lake (25th=4.48, 50th=8.11, 75th=10.25 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The rock bass historical average catch rate is 7.59 fish/gillnet for Green Lake. The 2008 rock bass average size was moderate (0.40 pounds and 7.63 inches) from gillnets. The rock bass historical average weight is 0.43 pounds from gillnets.

Largemouth bass numbers were moderate to abundant in 2008 (0.92 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for Green (25th=0.00, 50th=0.13, 75th=0.50 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The largemouth bass historical average catch rate is 0.53 fish/gillnet for Green. The 2008 largemouth bass average size was moderate (0.87 pounds and 11.18 inches) from gillnets. The largemouth bass historical average weight is 0.65 pounds from gillnets. Largemouth bass growth rates were generally below the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-6, but within the normal ranges for ages 7-8. The 2005 largemouth bass year class comprised 46% of the 2008 largemouth bass assessment gillnet and trapnet catch. YOY largemouth bass abundance was low (2.40 fish/seine/haul, 1.83 inches average length) in the 2008 shoreline seine hauls.

Current fish management activities on Green Lake include monitoring the fish population on an annual basis, protecting aquatic vegetation through the permit process, participating in local watershed initiatives to maintain or improve water quality, and stocking walleye to meet long-range goals. The Green Lake fishery will be sampled in the 2009 fall for walleye natural reproduction, and 2009 summer for all fish species by survey nets. ?
