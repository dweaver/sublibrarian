Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Fish Consumption Guidelines

Status of the Fishery (as of 07/12/2006)

A population assessment of Lake Wakanda was conducted in mid July of 2006. Wakanda is a large (1,664 acres), shallow (15 feet maximum), aerated, and productive lake located in Kandiyohi County. Partial winterkills of fish occurred during the mid to late 1990s due to high snowfall events or operational problems with the aeration system. Water levels were below normal during the 2006 summer due to drought like conditions. Submergent vegetation densities are variable in Wakanda. Sago pondweed is usually the most frequently occurring submergent species. Blue-green algae blooms are often intense and common during mid to late summer months. Wakanda receives moderate to high levels of agricultural and storm sewer runoff via tiles and ditches. Water clarity is often good from ice out to mid June, but poor during the remainder of the summer. Residential development is limited to the south shore of Wakanda. Walleye fry are generally stocked every other year or after years of poor fry survival. Local residents and various government officials are developing a new comprehensive management plan for Wakanda.

Black crappie numbers were low in 2006 (0.87 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The 2006 black crappie average size was moderate to large (0.77 pounds and 10.22 inches from trapnets). The black crappie historical average length is 8.93 inches from trapnets.

Bluegill numbers were moderate to high in 2006 (8.27 fish/trapnet) and within the normal range for similar lakes. The bluegill catch rate in 2006 was the highest recorded value for Wakanda. The 2006 bluegill average size was small (0.10 pounds and 4.48 inches from trapnets).

Northern pike numbers were moderate in 2006 (3.00 fish/gillnet) and within the normal range for similar lakes. The Wakanda northern pike historical average catch rate is 8.29 fish/gillnet. The 2006 northern pike average size was moderate (2.74 pounds and 22.67 inches from gillnets). The northern pike historical average length is 21.56 inches from gillnets. Northern pike growth rates in Wakanda were above or within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-6. The 2005, 2002, and 2000 year classes comprised the majority of the northern pike captured in the 2006 summer assessment.

Yellow perch numbers were moderate in 2006 (14.50 fish/gillnet) and within the normal range for similar lakes, but slightly below the Wakanda historical average catch rate (18.40 fish/gillnet). The 2006 yellow perch average size was moderate to large (0.39 pounds and 8.91 inches from gillnets). The yellow perch historical average length is 7.89 inches from gillnets. Yellow perch growth rates were above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-4.

Walleye numbers were abundant in 2006 (62.00 fish/gillnet) and above the normal range for similar lakes. The 2006 walleye catch rate was the highest recorded value for Wakanda. The 2006 walleye average size was moderate (1.53 pounds and 16.10 inches from gillnets). The walleye historical average length is 14.77 inches from gillnets. Walleye were also abundant in the 2006 trapnet sets (6.80 fish/trapnet, average length=16.43 inches). The 2006 catch rate of quality size (15.00 inches) and larger walleye was high (48.50 fish/gillnet). Walleye growth rates for age 1-8 were near the lower end of the Spicer Area normal ranges. The majority of walleye captured in the 2006 assessment were from the 2004 and 2002 year classes. Local anglers reported excellent success catching walleye (1-2 pounds) during the early ice-fishing seasons of 2004-2006.

Walleye natural reproduction in Wakanda is generally rare based on previous fall and summer surveys. Young of year YOY walleye numbers were abundant (88.57 YOY/hour, 5.18 inches average length) in the 2006 fall electrofishing survey (fry stocked year). The Spicer Area YOY walleye historical average catch rate is 70.35 YOY/hour from lakes fry stocked and lakes with natural reproduction combined. Walleye fry have been stocked recently into Wakanda during 1994, 1996, 1998, 2000, 2002, 2004, and 2006. The highest fall electrofishing catch rate of YOY walleye occurred in 1996 (284.00 YOY/hour, 7.23 inches average length) for a fry stocked year.

Black bullhead were numbers moderate to abundant in 2006 (58.00 fish/gillnet), but within the normal range for similar lakes. The Wakanda black bullhead historical average catch rate is 88.67 fish/gillnet. The 2006 black bullhead average size was moderate (0.30 pounds and 7.88 inches from gillnets). The black bullhead historical average length is 7.39 inches from gillnets.

Carp numbers were abundant in 2006 (55.50 fish/gillnet) and above the normal range for similar lakes. The carp abundance in the 2006 trapnets was also high (10.73 fish/trapnet, 3.26 pounds average weight). The 2006 carp average size was small (2.10 pounds and 16.10 inches from gillnets). The carp historical average length is 11.70 inches from gillnets.

Channel catfish numbers were moderate to high in 2006 (1.73 fish/trapnet), and the highest recorded value for Wakanda. The 2006 channel catfish average size was small (0.36 pounds and 6.78 inches from trapnets). Channel catfish have never been stocked in Wakanda by the DNR. However, downstream lakes like Big Kandiyohi have moderate channel catfish numbers and periodic stockings.

Current fish management activities on Wakanda include assisting Kandiyohi County in the operation of the aeration system, monitoring winter dissolved oxygen levels, protecting aquatic vegetation through the permit process, and stocking walleye fry every other year. The Lake Wakanda fishery is scheduled for survey in the 2011 summer for all fish species. Fall electrofishing surveys will be conducted during walleye fry stocked years to assess the success or failure of the stockings. ?
