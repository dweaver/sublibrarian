Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Fish Consumption Guidelines

Status of the Fishery (as of 07/09/2008)

A fish population assessment of Bass Lake was conducted in July of 2008. Bass is a small (52 acres) and moderately productive lake located in Kandiyohi County. The maximum depth is 31 feet. Residential development has occurred primarily along the north and west shoreline areas. Hardwoods dominate the east and south shorelines. Agricultural row crop drainage into the lake occurs via several inlets/tiles located along the west shore. Water levels were normal to high in 2008. A partial winterkill (primarily gamefish) occurred during the 1996-97 winter. Water levels were down 2-3 feet in 2003 due to drought conditions and the partial collapse and erosion of a crude (rock/log) private outlet structure. The outlet structure was repaired in 2006 by SWCD, county, and state. The outlet connects to Tait's Lake and eventually to Diamond Lake via the northern pike spawning area. Fish movement upstream into Bass Lake has occurred during high water events, especially during spring runoff. A township public access with limited parking and turn around is located along the north shore. Emergent vegetation (bulrush) is currently present along the east shoreline. Hardstem bulrush plants were transplanted into Bass Lake along the northeast and southwest corners of the lake in June of 1994. Water clarity was fair to poor (3.0 feet) in 2008. Algae blooms can occur during the late summer months. Moderate submergent vegetation was present in 2008. Submergent vegetation densities (pondweed species) have been historically variable in the lake. Dissolved oxygen was low (<0.6 ppm) in water depths greater than 18 feet during a 2008 mid summer profile. Bass Lake is managed primarily for largemouth bass and secondarily for northern pike, bluegill, and black crappie.

Largemouth bass numbers were moderate (50.00 fish/hour) in the 2008 spring electrofishing survey compared to the normal range (25th=14.85, 50th=33.05, 75th=70.98 fish/hour) for Spicer Area lakes and similar to the Bass Lake historical average (47.97 fish/hour). The 2008 largemouth bass average size was large (2.39 pounds and 14.5 inches) from spring electrofishing. The catch rate of preferred size (15.00 inches) and larger largemouth bass was high (24.29 fish/hour) in 2008. Largemouth bass growth rates in Bass Lake were near the high end or above the Spicer Area normal range for ages 1-8. The 2004 year class comprised 29% of the 2008 total largemouth bass spring electrofishing catch. Largemouth bass adults were stocked (22 fish) in Bass Lake during the spring of 1997 following the 1996-97 winterkill. One smallmouth bass (3.78 fish/hour, 15.98 inches) was captured in the 2008 spring electrofishing survey.

Black crappie numbers were abundant in 2008 (9.60 fish/trapnet) compared to the Bass Lake normal range (25th=1.00, 50th=3.98, 75th=10.50 fish/trapnet). There were also abundant numbers of small black crappie captured in the 2008 gillnets (20.00 fish/gillnet, 0.06 pounds average weight). The 2008 black crappie average size was moderate (0.36 pounds and 8.44 inches) from trapnets. The black crappie historical average weight is 0.22 pounds from trapnets. The catch rate of quality size (8.00 inches) and larger black crappie was high to moderate (6.60 fish/trapnet) in 2008. Black crappie growth rates were generally within or above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-4. The 2005 black crappie year class comprised 36% of the 2008 black crappie catch. Black crappie adults were stocked into Bass during the spring of 1997 (121 fish) and 2000 (532 fish) following the 1996-97 winterkill.

Northern pike numbers were abundant in 2008 (10.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for both Bass Lake (25th=2.96, 50th=5.50, 75th=8.50 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The northern pike historical average catch rate is 5.98 fish/gillnet. The 2008 northern pike average size was moderate (2.90 pounds and 22.41 inches) from gillnets. The northern pike historical average weight is 3.06 pounds from gillnets. The 2008 catch rate of quality size (21.00 inches) and larger northern pike was high (5.00 fish/gillnet). Northern pike growth rates were within or above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-5. Approximately 36% of the northern pike captured in the 2008 gillnets were from the 2006 year class. Northern pike adults (48 fish) were stocked into Bass Lake during 1998.

Yellow perch numbers were moderate in 2008 (3.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for Bass Lake (25th=1.00, 50th=3.83, 75th=56.38 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The yellow perch historical average catch rate is 26.06 fish/gillnet. The 2008 yellow perch average length was moderate (6.58 inches) from gillnets and trapnets combined.

Walleye numbers were low in 2008 (1.00 fish/gillnet), but within the normal ranges for Bass Lake (25th=0.00, 50th=0.75, 75th=2.25 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The walleye historical catch rate is 1.56 fish/gillnet. The 2008 walleye average size was large (3.20 pounds and 20.08 inches) from gillnets. The walleye historical average weight is 2.35 pounds from gillnets.

Bluegill numbers were moderate in 2008 (28.00 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal ranges for Bass Lake (25th=15.15, 50th=29.83, 75th=61.53 fish/trapnet) and similar lakes. The bluegill historical average catch rate is 34.75 fish/trapnet. The 2008 bluegill average size was small (0.07 pounds and 4.33 inches) from trapnets. The bluegill historical average weight is 0.15 pounds from trapnets. The catch rate of quality size (6.00 inches) and larger bluegill was low (2.40 fish/trapnet) in 2008. Bluegill growth rates were within or above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-4. The 2006 year class comprised 80% of the 2008 total bluegill catch. Bluegill adults (2,415 fish) were stocked into Bass Lake during the spring of 1997 following the 1996-97 winterkill.

Black bullhead numbers were low to moderate in 2008 (12.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for Bass Lake (25th=1.50, 50th=17.67, 75th=28.88 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The 2008 trapnet black bullhead catch was moderate (10.00 fish/trapnet). The 2008 black bullhead average size was large (0.65 pounds and 10.46 inches) from gillnets. The 2008 black bullhead average weight and length were 0.61 pounds and 10.31 inches respectively from trapnets. The black bullhead historical average weights are 0.46 and 0.45 pounds from gillnets and trapnets respectively.

There were no carp or white sucker captured in the 2008 survey nets. The historical average catch rates were 0.71 carp/gillnet and 2.31 white sucker/gillnet.

Current fish management activities on Bass Lake include monitoring the fish population on a periodic basis, periodic winter oxygen testing, protecting aquatic vegetation through the permit process, and stocking various fish species as warranted. The Bass Lake fishery will be resurveyed in the 2013 spring and summer for all fish species. ?
