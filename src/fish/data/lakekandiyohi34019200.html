Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 07/01/2004)

A resurvey was conducted on Long Lake during early July of 2004. Long is a large (1,575 acres), shallow (16 feet maximum depth), aerated, and productive lake located in Kandiyohi County. The watershed is varied with undeveloped hardwoods, agricultural row crops, grasslands, wetlands, and residential development. There are 83 homes located along the lakeshore. There are two public access sites located along the lake (northeast, southwest). Water levels were below normal during the early spring of 2004, but returned to normal levels after 10 inches of rainfall fell during May of 2004. The water clarity was excellent during mid June of 2004 (secchi=10.58 feet). Blue-green algae blooms were severe during mid to late summer in Long Lake. The nutrient levels were high in Long Lake during 2004 (total phosphorus=0.064 ppm). Submergent vegetation was low to moderate in density. Submergent vegetation consisted primarily of sago pondweed, water moss, and filamentous algae. Emergent vegetation stands were scattered along the lakeshore. Emergent vegetation consisted primarily of hardstem bulrush and cattail species. There are several mid-water bulrush islands in Long Lake. A large cormorant rookery is located on the large island. There are nine small inlets and one large inlet (Ringo Lake Outlet) that enter Long Lake. The Long Lake outlet connects to East Solomon Lake. The DNR Spicer Area Fisheries Office manages Long Lake for walleye, northern pike, black crappie, yellow perch, and largemouth bass. Walleye fry are generally stocked into Long Lake every other year or during years that walleye eggs are taken for statewide hatching operations. If a fry stocking is a failure, then the lake will generally be stocked again with fry the following spring. Walleye fingerling stocking is contingent in Long Lake after successive fry stocking failures.

Largemouth bass numbers were low in the 2004 spring electrofishing survey (20.95 fish/hour) compared to the Spicer Area average (47.22 fish/hour), but above the Long Lake historical average (12.04 fish/hour). The 2004 largemouth bass average size was large (2.42 pounds and 14.67 inches) from spring electrofishing. The 2004 catch rate of largemouth bass 15.00 inches and larger was moderate (9.52 fish/hour) in Long Lake. Largemouth bass growth rates in Long Lake were above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-7. The 2001 largemouth bass year class comprised 50% of the 2004 largemouth bass spring electrofishing catch.

Yellow perch numbers were moderate in 2004 (7.20 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, but below the Long Lake yellow perch historical average catch rate (13.13 fish/gillnet). The 2004 yellow perch average size was moderate (0.21 pounds and 7.03 inches) from gillnets. Yellow perch growth rates in Long Lake were above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-3. The 2003 yellow perch year class comprised 74% of the 2004 resurvey yellow perch catch.

Northern pike numbers were moderate in 2004 (3.80 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The northern pike historical average catch rate is 1.25 fish/gillnet for Long Lake. The 2004 northern pike average size was moderate (3.14 pounds and 24.41 inches) from gillnets. Northern pike growth rates in Long Lake were above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-5. The 2001 northern pike year class comprised 35% of the 2004 resurvey northern pike catch.

Bluegill numbers were low in 2004 (0.25 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes and the Long Lake bluegill historical average catch rate (1.68 fish/trapnet). The 2004 bluegill average size was small (0.13 pounds and 5.63 inches) from trapnets. Bluegill growth rates for Long Lake were above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-2. All bluegill captured in the 2004 resurvey were from the 2002 year class.

Black crappie numbers were low in 2004 (0.80 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The black crappie historical average catch rate is 0.26 fish/gillnet for Long. The 2004 black crappie average size was small (0.22 pounds and 6.54 inches) from gillnets. Black crappie growth rates in Long Lake were generally within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-3. The 2003 black crappie year class comprised 67% of the 2004 black crappie resurvey catch.

Walleye numbers were moderate in 2004 (6.20 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, but low compared to the Long Lake walleye historical average catch rate (20.65 fish/gillnet). The 2004 walleye average size was moderate (1.91 pounds and 16.10 inches) from gillnets. The 2004 catch rate of walleye 15.00 inches and larger was moderate (3.40 fish/gillnet) in Long Lake. Walleye growth rates in Long Lake were generally above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 2-8. The 2002 walleye year class comprised 34% of the 2004 resurvey walleye catch. Walleye natural reproduction in Long is frequent, but variable for numbers produced.

A fall electrofishing survey of Long Lake was conducted on September 27, 2004 to evaluate a 2004 spring walleye fry stocking (1,116,950 fry). There were abundant young of year "YOY" walleye numbers (117.00 YOY/hour, 5.39 inches average length) captured in 2004. Other area walleye fry stocked lakes had generally low YOY walleye catch rates in 2004 fall electrofishing surveys. The Long Lake fall electrofishing historical average catch rate and length are 58.63 YOY/hour and 6.48 inches respectively. Walleye fry were also stocked into Long Lake during 1994, 1998, 2000, 2001, 2002, and 2003. The highest fall electrofishing catch rate of YOY walleye occurred in 1994 (177.00 YOY/hour, 7.39 inches average length) for a fry stocked year. The highest fall electrofishing catch rate of YOY walleye due to natural reproduction occurred in 1995 (106.15 YOY/hour, 7.47 inches average length).

Black bullhead numbers were moderate in 2004 (7.60 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, but low compared to the Long Lake black bullhead historical average catch rate (126.34 fish/gillnet). The 2004 black bullhead average size was moderate (0.24 pounds and 7.36 inches) from gillnets.

Carp numbers were high in 2004 (6.33 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The carp historical average catch rate is 1.87 fish/gillnet for Long Lake. The 2004 carp average size was large to moderate (6.06 pounds and 22.68 inches) from gillnets.

Current fish management activities on Long Lake include monitoring the fish population on a periodic basis, monitoring winter dissolved oxygen levels, assisting the county with the operation of the aeration system, protecting aquatic vegetation through the permit process, participating in local watershed initiatives, and stocking various fish species as warranted. The Long Lake fishery will be sampled in the 2008 summer for all fish species.
