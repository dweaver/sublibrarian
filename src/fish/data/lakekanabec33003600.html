* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 07/09/2007)

Fish Lake is a 407 acre impoundment of the Ann River created by a 50 foot long dam. The walleye population is maintained through biannual fry/fingerling stocking, although natural reproduction does occur. Walleye fry have been stocked every other year in Fish Lake since 1994. Walleye fingerlings were stocked in 1989, 1990, and 1992. Fish Lake is assigned to lake class 42. Lakes grouped in class 42 are typically shallow and turbid with hard water. The current management goal is to attain a walleye gill net catch between 2.0 and 6.0 per gill net and northern pike between 1.0 and 3.0 per gill net. A population assessment was conducted on Fish Lake the summer of 2007 in order to assess current fish populations and management goals.

The walleye gillnet catch (2.3/set) was within the normal range compared to other catches in the lake class and increased from the 2002 survey catch of (1.0/set). Walleye averaged 1.8 pounds, which is a decline from 2.7 average pounds in the 2002 survey. The walleye catch was within the management goal of 2.0 to 6.0 fish per net. Sampled walleye ranged from 7.2 to 21.3 inches with a 16.7 inch mean. Walleye size structure has decreased from the 19.9 inch average in the 2002 survey. While walleye growth was slowed down compared to past surveys, it is still in the 50th quartile when compared to the lake class norm. The 2007 population assessment has shown the walleye population in Fish Lake to be slowly improving when compared to past surveys. Although there is some variance in year class strength from year to year the overall status of the walleye fishery in Fish Lake is in good condition.

The northern pike gillnet catch of 6.7/ set exceeded the long range management goal of 1.0 to 3.0 pike per gill net. Sampled northerns ranged from 17.4 to 32.5 inches with a 23.2 inch average. Northern pike catch rates decreased from 8.8/set in 2002 to 6.7/set in 2007. Average length of northern pike is up from 22.1 inches in 2002 to 23.2 inches in 2007. The northern pike maximum length slightly increased from 30.1 inches in 2002 to 32.5 inches in 2007. Average weight has slightly decreased from the 2002 survey but is in the 50th quartile for lakes in this class. Growth and ageing data analysis from northern pike scales displayed six year classes from 2000 through 2005. The growth rate is within the normal range when compared to other lakes in the same class. By all accounts the northern pike fishery in Fish Lake is presently healthy with good numbers and showing great signs of quality angling.

Spring electrofishing for largemouth bass sampled 26 bass. Catch per unit effort increased from 10.5 fish per hour in 2002 to 31.3 fish per hour in 2007. Total weight of the bass sampled in 2002 was 17.5 pounds compared to 48.3 in 2007. The size structure of largemouth bass sampled in 2007 ranged from 6.5 inches to 21 inches. Aging data revealed nine year classes (3-11) from 1996 to 2004. Length at age data portrays a moderate and steady growth rate for largemounth bass in Fish Lake. With excellent catch rates, good growth rates, and consistent recruitment, the outlook for largemouth bass in Fish Lake is in good condition and should remain healthy for years to come.

Black crappie numbers in the Fish Lake 2007 population assessment have significantly increased from the 2002 survey. Gill net catch rates for black crappie increased from 7.2 fish per set in 2002 to 48.5 fish per set in 2007. The 48.5 fish per net is the highest catch rate ever recorded in Fish Lake. The average weight of black crappie was .33 lbs and above the 75th quartile for lakes in this class. Black crappie sampled ranged from 4.2 to 11.3 inches with a 8.0 inch mean, which indicates an excellent size structure. The high numbers and exceptional size structure of black crappie will create a first class black crappie fishery in Fish Lake for years to come.

The bluegill trapnetting was in the 50th quartile compared to others in the lake class with the catch rate of (14.4/set). Bluegill numbers have increased from 9.6 fish per net in 2002 to 14.4 fish per set in 2007. The average size of 0.25 pounds has decreased compared to the 2001 survey of .33 pounds, but is in the 50th quartile for lakes in this class. Sampled bluegill ranged from 5.3 to 11.1 inches with a 8.2 inch mean. Bluegill gillnetting sampled (3.8/set) and ranged from 4.2 to 11.3 inches with a 7.8 inch mean. There is a good number and even distribution of bluegills in Fish Lake, which will produce a good number of quality size fish for anglers.

Yellow perch numbers were below the 25th quartile for this lake class. Yellow perch catch rate decreased from (4.7/gillnet) in 2002 to (2.5/gillnet) in 2007. The average weight of 0.23 pounds was in the 50% quartile for the lake class. Yellow perch are an important forage fish and numbers may be declining due to predation from other gamefish populations.

The channel catfish catch rate was 1.3 fish per net set in 2007, which is similar to past surveys. Length of catfish ranged form 20.1 to 28.2 inches and the average length was 22.8 inches. Although there is not a high number of channel catfish in Fish Lake, the size structure is good.

Trap net sets sampled 37 common carp in 2007, compared to 6 sampled in 2002. Common carp abundance has increased from .67 fish per net in 2002 to 4.11 fish per net in 2007. Carp lengths ranged from 18.9 to 32.8 inches. The 37 carp sampled in 2007 is the highest catch for common carp in Fish Lake since the 1979 survey, in which 39 carp were captured.

White crappies were absent from the Fish Lake survey in 2007. Eight white crappies were caught during the 2002 survey.

Other fish species sampled during the 2007 Fish Lake survey were shorthead redhorse, silver redhorse, white sucker, yellow bullhead, bowfin, brown bullhead, pumpkinseed, freshwater drum, and greater redhorse.
