Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 07/11/2005)

A fish population assessment of Andrew Lake was conducted in July of 2005. Andrew is a moderately sized (814 acres), moderately deep (26 feet maximum), and low to moderately productive lake located in Kandiyohi County. Sibley State Park is located along the north and northwest shoreline areas. Shores of St. Andrew's Bible Camp is located along the east shore. Residential development has occurred throughout the entire shoreline area with the exception of the northwest portion of the lake. DNR public access sites are located along the south side and northwest side (Sibley State Park) of the lake. Emergent vegetation (bulrush) is abundant along the northwest bay and north shore areas in Andrew. Submergent vegetation densities within the lake are also abundant within the littoral zone areas. Andrew is connected to several periodic winterkill (Henchien, Swan, Middle) and non-winterkill lakes (Norway, Games, Florida) via the Shakopee Creek and various small inlets. Water clarity (secchi=7.83 feet) was moderate in 2005. Andrew is managed primarily for walleye, northern pike, largemouth bass, black crappie, and bluegill. Walleye stockings (primarily fingerlings) are scheduled for every other year in Andrew.

Black crappie numbers were low in 2005 (0.33 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The black crappie gillnet historical average catch rate was 2.63 fish/gillnet for Andrew. The 2005 black crappie average size was moderate (0.67 pounds and 10.3 inches) from gillnets. The black crappie historical average weight is 0.33 pounds from gillnets.

Northern pike numbers were abundant in 2005 (8.50 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The northern pike historical average catch rate is 4.75 fish/gillnet for Andrew. The 2005 northern pike average size was small to moderate (2.19 pounds and 21.6 inches) from gillnets. The northern pike historical average weight is 2.15 pounds from gillnets. The 2005 catch rate of quality size (21.0 inches and larger) northern pike was high (4.17 fish/gillnet) in Andrew. Northern pike growth rates in Andrew were generally below the Spicer Area normal ranges. The largest northern pike captured was 33.7 inches.

Yellow perch numbers were low to moderate in 2005 (11.83 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The yellow perch historical average catch rate is 24.02 fish/gillnet for Andrew. The 2005 yellow perch average size was small (0.10 pounds and 5.9 inches) from gillnets. The yellow perch historical average weight is 0.14 pounds from gillnets.

Walleye numbers were abundant in 2005 (9.17 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The walleye historical average catch rate is 6.02 fish/gillnet for Andrew. The 2005 walleye average size was moderate (1.78 pounds and 16.2 inches) from gillnets. The walleye historical average weight is 1.72 pounds from gillnets. The 2005 catch rate of quality size (15.0 inches and larger) walleye (3.83 fish/gillnet) was moderate and similar to the Andrew historical average (3.53 fish/gillnet). Walleye growth rates were generally within the Spicer Area normal ranges. The walleye population in Andrew is due primarily to stocking. Limited walleye natural reproduction has been documented by past fall electrofishing surveys. Andrew was recently stocked with walleye in the 2005 fall (8,268 fingerlings, 536 pounds; 820 yearlings, 349 pounds; 6 adults, 6 pounds). The 2002 walleye year class (stocked and natural reproduction) comprised 32% of the walleye catch in the 2005 assessment. The largest walleye captured was 28.5 inches from the 2005 assessment.

Bluegill numbers were low to moderate in 2005 (11.75 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The bluegill historical average catch rate is 31.69 fish/trapnet in Andrew. The 2005 bluegill average size was moderate (0.16 pounds and 5.6 inches) from trapnets. The bluegill historical average weight is 0.18 pounds from trapnets. The 2005 catch rate of quality size (6.0 inches and larger) bluegill was low (5.50 fish/trapnet) in Andrew.

Largemouth bass numbers were moderate in 2005 (0.50 fish/trapnet) trapnets compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The 2005 largemouth bass average size was small (5.1 inches) from trapnets. A spring electrofishing survey was not conducted for largemouth bass in 2005 due to time constraints. The last spring electrofishing survey for largemouth bass in Andrew was done in 2001. Abundant numbers of largemouth bass were captured in the Andrew 2001 spring electrofishing survey (114.00 fish/hour, 11.6 inches average length). The Andrew spring electrofishing largemouth bass historical average catch rate is 74.91 fish/hour.

Freshwater drum numbers were moderate in 2005 (4.83 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, but above the historical average catch rate (2.97 fish/gillnet) for Andrew. The 2005 freshwater drum average size was small (0.96 pounds and 12.8 inches) from gillnets. The freshwater drum historical average weight is 2.43 pounds from gillnets.

Current fish management activities on Andrew include monitoring the fish population on a periodic basis, protecting aquatic vegetation through the permit process, participating in local watershed initiatives, and stocking various fish species as warranted. The Andrew fishery will be surveyed in the 2010 spring for largemouth bass and 2010 summer for all fish species.
