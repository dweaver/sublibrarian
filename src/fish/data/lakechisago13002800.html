Status of the Fishery (as of 07/18/2005)

South Lindstrom Lake, which is located in southern Chisago County within the cities of Lindstrom and Chisago City, is a moderately fertile lake just under 500 acres in size. The shoreline is predominately sand with good water clarity given the area. The lake is broadly connected to Chisago Lake by a natural channel. The two lakes are somewhat physically different with South Lindstrom having less shallow water habitat. However, the fish species composition and abundance are very similar. The current management plan treats both lakes as one for management purposes with walleye and largemouth bass as the primary management.

A lake survey was conducted during the week of July 18, 2005, to evaluate the current fishery and the management goals for South Lindstrom Lake. Walleye abundance was high while average size, 17.8 inches and 2.3 pounds, were both above normal for this lake type. Walleye numbers have remained fairly consistent since 1990. Since natural reproduction has been limited, the walleye population has been maintained primarily through stocking. Spring night electrofishing found an excellent abundance of quality sized largemouth bass. Lengths extended from 6 to 18 inches. Along with Chisago Lake, South Lindstrom was selected for an experimental, 12 inch maximum largemouth bass regulation in 1997. A high bass population, consistent reproduction, and heavy fishing pressure were deciding factors. The goal of the regulation is to increase the proportion of bass greater than 13 inches. Prior to the regulation, only 14% of the sampled bass exceeded this mark. Post regulation sampling has shown a stepped increase in larger bass with 38% of the 2005 catch over 13 inches. Night electrofishing is scheduled biennially to evaluate the bass population. The abundance of northern pike fell in the normal range for the lake class but the average size, at 4.4 pounds, was higher than in most similar lakes. Measured northern ranged from 17.2 to 40.6 inches. Bluegill and black crappie were found at a normal abundance levels with a slight increase in size since 2000. Yellow perch, a valuable food source for northern pike and walleye, were found at a normal abundance in 2005 and appear to be on the increase.

During a fishing outing, anglers are likely to encounter an abundance of nice sized walleye, a good number of larger northern pike, or an abundance of quality sized largemouth bass. Good numbers of average sized bluegill and black crappie should keep panfish anglers interested. The current management goals set for South Lindstrom and Chisago Lakes combined are to develope and maintain a largemouth bass electrofishing catch rate of 110 fish/hour with the highest number of fish in the 13-15 inch range, a walleye gill net catch rate from 3-8 fish/lift with an average size near 2.0 pounds, and a northern pike gill net catch rate of 4.0 fish/lift with an average size near 3.0 pounds. With the exception of northern abundance, all management goals were met in 2005. The combined catch rate for largemouth bass was 123 per hour with a mode between 14 and 16 inches. The combined catch rate for walleye was 4.4/set with an average weight of 2.7 pounds. Northern pike abundance, 3.7/gillnet, fell just short of the goal but mean weight reached 4.7 pounds. Fishing enthusiasts should remember that harvest of largemouth bass at 12 inches or larger is prohibited and are encouraged to be selective if they keep fish by harvesting the small ones for eating and releasing the larger ones. Releasing the larger fish will help maintain or enhance the current quality of the fish population and provide future angling opportunities for others who would like to experience the excitement of hooking a quality sized fish.
