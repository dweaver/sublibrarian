* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 06/21/2007)

Lady Slipper is a small (247 acres), shallow (11 feet maximum), aerated, and productive lake located in Lyon County. A population assessment of Lady Slipper was conducted during mid June of 2007. Water clarity was fair to poor during mid June of 2007 (2.1 feet). Residential development is limited to two farm sites along the lake. Both partial summerkills and winterkills have occurred in Lady Slipper, but these have not occurred since the late 1990s. Blue-green algae blooms are often intense during the summer months. Lady Slipper receives moderate to high nutrient inputs from agricultural row crops and feedlots. Nearby School Grove Lake can connect to Lady Slipper via a tile/marsh area during high water conditions. Lady Slipper is scheduled for walleye fry stocking every other year. If a fry stocking is a failure or a winterkill occurs, then the lake will generally be stocked again with fry the following spring. Walleye fingerlings/yearlings may be stocked in Lady Slipper after two poor consecutive fall electrofishing surveys for YOY walleye. Lady Slipper can be a popular local walleye and yellow perch fishery. Lady Slipper is managed primarily for walleye with bluegill, yellow perch, and black bullhead as secondary species.

Black bullhead numbers were moderate in 2007 (37.56 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, but below the Lady Slipper historical average catch rate (125.35 fish/trapnet). The 2007 black bullhead gillnet catch rate (115.00/gillnet) was below the historical average (141.00 fish/gillnet). The 2007 black bullhead average size was moderate (0.41 pounds and 8.61 inches) from trapnets. The 2007 black bullhead average size was small to moderate (0.32 pounds and 7.53 inches) from gillnets. The black bullhead historical average weights were 0.29 pounds and 0.30 pounds respectively from gillnets and trapnets for Lady Slipper.

Carp were not present in the 2007 gillnets or trapnets. Carp have been captured in only one other Lady Slipper survey (2 carp from trapnets in 1997).

Bluegill numbers were low to moderate in 2007 (1.78 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The bluegill historical catch rate is 2.83 fish/trapnet for Lady Slipper. The 2007 bluegill average size was large (0.55 pounds and 7.96 inches) from trapnets. The bluegill historical average length is 7.40 inches from trapnets. The 2007 catch rate of preferred size (8.00 inches) and larger bluegill was moderate to low (1.44 fish/trapnet). Bluegill growth rates were above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-8. The 2000 year class comprised 36% of the 2007 bluegill catch.

Other sunfish species captured in Lady Slipper during 2007 included moderate to high numbers of green sunfish (2.22 fish/trapnet), hybrid sunfish (1.56 fish/trapnet), and orangespotted sunfish (19.00 fish/trapnet). The 2007 average lengths were 4.66 inches for green sunfish, 5.90 inches for hybrid sunfish, and 3.53 inches for orangespotted sunfish.

Yellow perch were abundant in 2007 (94.50 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes and above the Lady Slipper historical average catch rate (24.38 fish/gillnet). The 2007 yellow perch average length was moderate (7.93 inches) from gillnets. The yellow perch historical average length is 7.67 inches. The 2007 catch rate of preferred size (10 inches) and larger yellow perch was high (10.94 fish/gillnet). Yellow perch growth rates were above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-7. The 2005 year class comprised 51% of the 2007 yellow perch catch.

Northern pike numbers were low in 2007 (0.50 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. Northern pike have not been captured in previous Lady Slipper surveys. The northern pike average length was 21.85 inches from gillnets.

Walleye were abundant in 2007 (39.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes and above the Lady Slipper historical average catch rate (28.58 fish/gillnet). The 2007 walleye trapnet catch rate (4.78 fish/trapnet) was similar to the Lady Slipper historical average (5.39 fish/trapnet). The 2007 walleye average size was moderate (1.26 pounds and 15.20 inches) from gillnets. The 2007 walleye average size was moderate to large (2.12 pounds and 18.02 inches) from trapnets. The 2007 catch rate of quality size (15.00 inches) and larger walleye was high (21.50 fish/gillnet). Walleye growth rates were above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-2, within the normal ranges for ages 3-5, and below the normal ranges for ages 6-7. The 2005 (fry stocked) and 2003 year classes (fry and fingerling stocked) accounted for 48% and 28% respectively of the 2007 walleye catch.

Moderate numbers of young of year "YOY" walleye were present in the 2006 fall electrofishing survey (39.00 YOY/hour, 6.54 inches average length). There were no young of year "YOY" walleye captured in 2003. Low numbers of YOY walleye were captured in the 2002 (21.43 YOY/hour, 6.10 inches average length) and 2005 (18.00 YOY/hour, 6.93 inches average length) fall electrofishing surveys. The highest Lady Slipper fall electrofishing catch rate for YOY walleye occurred in 1998 (72.00 YOY/hour, 4.51 inches average length). The Lady Slipper fall electrofishing historical average catch rate and length were 30.09 YOY/hour and 6.02 inches respectively. The Spicer Area YOY walleye historical average catch rate is 71.24 YOY/hour from lakes fry stocked and lakes with natural reproduction. Walleye fry have been stocked recently into Lady Slipper during 1996, 1998, 2000, 2002, 2003, 2005, and 2006. Walleye fingerlings (5,506 fish, 622 pounds) were stocked into Lady Slipper during October of 2003 in response to the 2003 fry stocking failure.

Current fish management activities on Lady Slipper include monitoring the fish population on a periodic basis, monitoring winter dissolved oxygen levels, working with the local residents and clubs with the operation of the aeration system, protecting aquatic vegetation through the permit process, participating in local watershed initiatives, and stocking various fish species as warranted. The Lady Slipper Lake fishery will be sampled in the 2008 fall for YOY walleye numbers. ?
