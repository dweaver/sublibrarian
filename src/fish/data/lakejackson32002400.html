Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 06/16/2008)

Little Spirit is a 572-acre lake located in Jackson County, MN and Dickinson County, IA. Little Spirit has a watershed to lake ratio of only 6:1 that covers approximately 2,700 acres. Well over 200 acres of that watershed has restorable wetlands that are currently being used for row cropping. It is this high productivity that likely causes dense mats of algae and musty smell during mid-summer. Big Spirit Lake in Iowa is connected to Little Spirit through a grated culvert that restricts immigration and emigration of fish. With that said, muskellunge have been captured in Little Spirit, which were probably from an IA DNR Big Spirit muskie stocking. A helixor aeration system has been running each winter since 1992. In recent years (2004) during mid-summer, some summer kill has occurred concurrent with large algae blooms. This class 42 lake has a maximum depth of 8.0 feet and is managed primarily for walleye and secondarily for largemouth bass, black crappie, and yellow perch. A population assessment was conducted during the week of June 6, 2008 using 3 gill nets and 11 trap nets.

Walleye numbers rebounded slightly from the previous survey on Little Spirit. The catch rate for walleye in gill nets was 5.0 fish/set in 2008 compared to 1.8 fish/set in 2004. Total lengths of walleye sampled ranged from 9.2 to 19.0 inches with a mean of 16.9 inches. Only 2 years out of the last 8 years have walleye, either fry, small fingerling, or large fingerling, not been stocked in Little Spirit either by MN or IA DNR. Some nice eater walleyes can be caught.

Black crappie numbers have changed substantially since the 2004 survey. The catch rate for black crappie in trap nets was 0.27 fish/set in 2008 compared to 14.2 fish/set in 2004. Black crappie population abundance can be variable as success of spawning is negatively influenced by wind. The Windom Area lakes experience a great array of wind direction and speeds, which influence the variable populations. I would suggest targeting yellow perch before black crappie.

Yellow perch numbers appear to be on the rebound. Yellow perch are likely filling the panfish niche in Little Spirit. The average fish was around 5.8 inches long and needs a few years of growth to be worthy of a skillet. Although most of the perch were small, the numbers will likely provide some good angling action.

The gill net catch rate of black bullhead was 16 fish/set, which was within the expected range (9.6 to 91.4 fish/set). Our data suggests that the black bullhead numbers are low but larger fish dominate the population.

Other species sampled were bluegill, common carp, freshwater drum, longnose gar, northern pike, shortnose gar, white sucker, and yellow bullhead.

Efforts to improve the health of the lake should focus on best management practices within the watershed. Improvements and education in land stewardship will often have secondary benefits to the lake in the way of quality and quantity of habitat. Generally, improvements in the quality and quantity of lake habitat will mean direct improvements of the fishery.
