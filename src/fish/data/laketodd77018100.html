Status of the Fishery (as of 06/21/2004)

Maple Lake is a popular fishing lake in Todd County and provides plenty of angling opportunity. Known for the bluegill and black crappie fishing, the lake also has walleye, northern pike, and largemouth bass. Much of the shoreline is sand except for the west side that is more marshy. Coontail and filamentous algae were the most common submerged aquatic vegetation with cattails being the most common emergent plant. Water quality has been an on-going problem for Maple Lake as summer algae blooms are common. The secchi disk reading of 8 feet at the time of the survey in June was good but by August, water clarity usually has been reduced to 2 feet. Some major changes were observed in the fish community composition since the last survey in 2001. Walleye numbers were up after being at a record low in 2001 but are still slightly below management goals for the lake. While there appears to be some keeper size walleye, most of the fish in the survey were over 20 inches in length, with smaller fish under 12 inches being scarce. Walleye fingerlings are stocked in the lake in odd numbered years and the high northern pike numbers observed in the previous survey may have affected the survival of the 2003 fingerlings. Northern pike numbers did show a decline from 2001 and fall within the range of management goals for the lake. Anglers have a good chance of catching a nice pike as the average size in the survey was around 3.5 pounds with some fish in the 11 pound range. Typical methods of assessing largemouth bass were not used in this survey, however, Maple Lake has a history of producing big bass. An ice-out assessment conducted in 2004 did catch some bass that were in the 4 - 5 pound range. Yellow perch also appeared to be effected by the high pike population as a decline in numbers was seen in the 2004 survey, however, histortical netting data indicate that the species has the ability to quickly rebound when pike numbers decline. An abundant perch population is an important component in maintaining good growth rates in both northern pike and walleye. Perch have also been found to be instrumental in maintaining a well-balanced bluegill population of fast growing, quality individuals. Maple Lake has a reputation for producing some good bluegill fishing and quality fish. Abundance declined from the last survey but almost a quarter of the fish were over 7 inches in length. Black crappie abundance also declined and the population appears to be dominated by small fish under 9 inches but some individuals up to 12 inches were observed. Pumpkinseeds are a sunfish species in the lake that appear to be numerous and slightly smaller in size than the bluegills. Maple Lake's roughfish community is comprised of white suckers, shorthead redhorse, bowfin or dogfish, black bullheads, brown bullheads, and yellow bullheads. Yellow bullheads were the most numerous of the three species and all three populations had fish over 13 inches in length. Maple Lake offers some excellent fishing opportunity. Releasing larger northern pike and walleye and voluntarily limiting harvest of large panfish will help sustain the quality and balance of the fish community. Good agricultural and shoreline management practices are encouraged in the surrounding watershed to improve water quality. The Todd County Planning and Zoning office in Long Prairie has a good website which provides information to shoreland property owners. (www.co.todd.mn.us/PlanningZoning/planning.htm)
