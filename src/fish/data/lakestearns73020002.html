Fish Stocking Activity

Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Fish Consumption Guidelines

No fish consumption guidelines are available for this lake. For more information, see the "Fish Consumption Advice" pages at the Minnesota Department of Health.


Status of the Fishery (as of 07/25/2007)

A resurvey of Lake Koronis was conducted in late July of 2007. Koronis is a large, deep, and productive lake located primarily in Stearns County. Koronis is a popular fishery for walleye, northern pike, smallmouth bass, and bluegill. Koronis receives moderate recreational use during the summer months. There are five public access sites on Koronis. The lake is highly developed with approximately 498 homes and cabins, and seven resorts/bible camp/campgrounds. In addition, the city of Paynesville is within two miles of the lake. The immediate watershed is composed primarily of agricultural row crops, hardwood areas, and residential development. Nutrient runoff enters Koronis via 45 inlets/tiles (mostly small inlets) from agricultural, city storm sewer, and lake residential sources. The largest inlet is the North Fork of the Crow River, which enters Koronis along the east shore and outlets near the southeast corner. The greatest nutrient loading volume is delivered to Koronis via the North Fork of the Crow River. Nutrient levels (total phosphorus=0.030 ppm, chlorophyll A=24.0 ppb) were moderate in June of 2007 for Koronis. Water clarity was fair (3.5  5.0 feet) during the 2007 summer in Koronis. Water temperatures were stratified by mid June through late August of 2007. Low (<1.0 ppm) or no oxygen was present in depths below 30 feet during the 2007 summer. No summerkills of tullibee (cisco) or white suckers were noted of any consequence in 2007, however partial summerkills are common during hot summers. Best management practices (buffers, storm sewer settling ponds, better wastewater management practices, shore land practices) have been enacted or encouraged by local governments, North Fork Crow River Watershed District, homeowners, lake associations, and sportsmans club. Bank erosion is noticeable along steep clay banks of First Island and two large points (Petersons and Stoney Point) along the eastern portion of the lake.

Aquatic vegetation densities are variable yearly in the lake. Submergent vegetation species that occurred frequently and had high mean abundance in the 2007 transects included wild celery, sago pondweed, muskgrass (chara), coontail, northern milfoil, water moss, and clasping-leaf pondweed. Filamentous algae were also abundant during 2007. Hardstem bulrush stands were limited to the west side of Second Island, southeast area of the outlet bay, and Hendrickson creek area. There are three large islands located on Koronis. Shoalwater substrates are varied with boulder, rubble, gravel, sand, and silt. Water levels were below normal during the summer due to drought conditions, but improved by late fall in 2007. Koronis is a periodic walleye egg take site for the statewide walleye propagation program. Koronis is managed primarily for walleye and northern pike, in addition to secondary species such as bluegill, yellow perch, cisco, bass, and black crappie.

Black crappie numbers were high in 2007 (7.13 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes and the Koronis black crappie historical average catch rate (0.71 fish/gillnet). The 2007 black crappie average size was small to moderate (0.19 pounds and 7.01 inches) from gillnets. The black crappie historical average length is 8.29 inches from gillnets. Black crappie growth rates were above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1 and 3-4, and within the normal range for age 2. The 2005 year class comprised 98% of the 2007 black crappie gillnet and trapnet catch in Koronis.

Bluegill numbers were moderate in 2007 (18.20 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, but above the Koronis bluegill historical average catch rate (9.07 fish/trapnet). The 2007 bluegill average size was small (0.07 pounds and 4.58 inches) from the trapnets. The bluegill historical average length is 4.90 inches from trapnets. The 2007 catch rate of quality size (6.00 inches) and larger bluegill was low (1.20 fish/trapnet). Young of year "YOY" bluegill catch rates were high (59.89 YOY/haul, 1.32 inches average length) in the 2007 shoreline seine hauls. Bluegill growth rates were generally above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-4. The 2005 year class comprised 83% of the 2007 bluegill gillnet and trapnet catch.

Northern pike numbers were abundant in 2007 (9.88 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes and above the Koronis northern pike historical average catch rate (2.70 fish/gillnet). The 2007 northern pike average size was moderate (3.26 pounds and 23.52 inches) from gillnets. The northern pike historical average length is 23.27 inches from gillnets. The 2007 catch rate of preferred size (28.00 inches) and larger northern pike was low to moderate (1.00 fish/gillnet). Northern pike growth rates in Koronis were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-6. The 2005 and 2004 year classes comprised 49% and 28% of the 2007 northern pike gillnet and trapnet catch. The largest northern pike captured in the 2007 resurvey was 35.83 inches.

Smallmouth bass numbers were high in 2007 (1.88 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes and above the smallmouth bass historical average catch rate (0.69 fish/gillnet) for Koronis. The 2007 smallmouth bass average size was moderate (1.55 pounds and 12.93 inches) from gillnets. The smallmouth bass historical average length is 12.72 inches from gillnets. The 2007 catch rate of preferred size (14.00 inches) and larger smallmouth bass was high (0.63 fish/gillnet) compared to the Koronis historical average (0.36 fish/gillnet). Shoreline seine catch rates of YOY smallmouth bass were low (0.89 fish/haul, 2.52 inches average length) in 2007. Smallmouth bass growth rates were above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 3-6, but within the normal ranges for ages 1-2. The 2005 smallmouth bass year class comprised 47% of the 2007 smallmouth bass gillnet and trapnet catch. The largest smallmouth bass captured in the 2007 resurvey was 18.90 inches.

Spring electrofishing surveys were not conducted for largemouth bass or smallmouth bass in 2007 due to workload constraints. Local anglers reported good success catching largemouth and smallmouth bass during the 2007 summer. Largemouth bass abundance in the Koronis 2003 spring electrofishing survey was low (15.27 fish/hour). The 2003 largemouth bass average weight and length were moderate (1.22 pounds and 12.22 inches) from spring electrofishing. The Spicer Area historical average largemouth bass catch rate is 46.6 fish/hour from spring electrofishing. Previous spring electrofishing largemouth bass catch rates for Koronis were 36.67 fish/hour in 1990 (0.55 lbs, 9.2 inches average size) and 26.40 fish/hour in 1991 (0.60 pounds, 10.0 inches average size).

Tullibee "cisco" numbers were moderate to high in 2007 (1.88 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes and above the tullibee historical average catch rate (1.19 fish/gillnet) for Koronis. The 2007 tullibee average size was small (0.69 pounds and 10.82 inches) from gillnets. The tullibee historical average length is 12.32 inches from gillnets. Tullibee growth rates were below the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-6. The 2005 and 2003 year classes comprised 47% and 33% of the 2007 summer gillnet catch. Tullibee are an important forage species for both large northern pike and walleye.

Yellow perch numbers were low in 2007 (9.88 fish/gillnet) compared to normal range for similar lakes and below the yellow perch historical average catch rate (61.80 fish/gillnet) for Koronis. The 2007 yellow perch average size was moderate (0.24 pounds and 7.93 inches) from gillnets. The yellow perch historical average length is 7.02 inches from gillnets. The 2007 catch rate of quality size (8.00 inches) and larger yellow perch was low to moderate (5.50 fish/gillnet). The shoreline seine haul catch of YOY yellow perch was low to moderate (8.22 fish/haul, 2.35 inches average length) in 2007. Yellow perch growth rates were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-2, and above the normal ranges for ages 3-6. The 2004 year class comprised 37% of the 2007 yellow perch gillnet and trapnet catch.

Walleye numbers were high in 2007 (10.25 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes and above the walleye historical average catch rate (8.96 fish/gillnet) for Koronis. The 2007 walleye average size was moderate (1.30 pounds and 14.58 inches) from gillnets. The walleye historical average length is 13.83 inches from gillnets. The 2007 catch rate of quality size (15.00 inches) and larger walleye (4.50 fish/gillnet) was higher than the historical average (3.09 fish/gillnet) for Koronis. Walleye growth rates were generally below the Spicer Area normal quartiles for ages 1-8. The 2006 and 2004 year classes (stocked and natural reproduction) comprised 28% and 19% of the 2007 walleye gillnet and trapnet catch. The 2005 year class comprised 21% of the 2007 walleye gillnet and trapnet catch.

Walleye natural reproduction in Koronis was generally both frequent and adequate to sustain walleye numbers based on previous fall and summer surveys from 1993-2002. YOY Walleye numbers were low (<11.0 YOY/hour) from 2003-2006 in Koronis. Walleye fingerlings were stocked in 2004 (2,409 pounds, 48,180 fish) and a combination of fingerlings/yearlings/adults (2,355 pounds, 45,685 fish) were stocked in 2006 due to low YOY numbers observed in fall electrofishing surveys. Walleye fry were stocked during 1996, and 2001-2007 as a 10% return of walleye eggs taken for the DNR statewide walleye-hatching program. There were moderate to high numbers of YOY walleye (44.00 YOY/hour, 7.29 inches average length) from fry stocking and natural reproduction in the 2007 fall electrofishing survey. A strong 2001 year class from natural reproduction (79%) and stocking of oxytetracycline marked fry (21%) was documented in the Koronis 2001 fall electrofishing survey (84.92 YOY/hour). The fall electrofishing YOY walleye historical average catch rate is 29.35 YOY walleye/hour for Koronis.

Rock bass numbers were moderate in 2007 (2.25 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The rock bass historical average catch rate is 1.46 fish/gillnet for Koronis. The 2007 rock bass average size was moderate to small (0.25 pounds and 6.51 inches) from gillnets. The rock bass historical average length is 7.15 inches from gillnets.

Other species of interest captured in 2007 include low numbers of black bullhead (2.63 fish/gillnet) and carp (0.13 fish/trapnet) from Koronis. The Koronis historical average catch rate for black bullhead is 19.82 fish/gillnet. The Koronis historical average catch rate for carp is 1.63 fish/trapnet.

Current fish management activities on Koronis include protecting the important aquatic vegetation such as bulrush through the permit process, participating in local watershed projects, stocking various species as needed, and stocking walleye fingerlings after two consecutive years of poor natural reproduction as documented by fall night electrofishing surveys. The Koronis fishery will be surveyed again for YOY walleye in the 2008 fall. ?
