Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Fish Consumption Guidelines

No fish consumption guidelines are available for this lake. For more information, see the "Fish Consumption Advice" pages at the Minnesota Department of Health.


Status of the Fishery (as of 06/19/2006)

A resurvey of Lake Ella was conducted during mid June of 2006. Ella is a small (136 acres), shallow (12 feet maximum depth), and productive lake located in Kandiyohi County. Several springs are located along the north shore. Partial winterkills have occurred in Ella, but are generally rare. Water levels were below normal during 2006. High nutrient runoff inflows are common into Ella during rain events from agricultural sources via the shoreline areas, tiles, and ditches. The outlet connects to Lake Elizabeth during moderate to high water levels. There were high levels of total phosphorus (0.060 ppm) and chlorophyll-a (17.5 ppb) in 2006. The water clarity was poor (secchi disc=2.20 feet) during the 2006 resurvey. High densities of submergent vegetation (curly-leaf pondweed) were present during the spring and summer of 2002, however densities were low during 2006. Low densities of submergents included sago pondweed, water moss, and coontail. Broad-leaved cattail was a common emergent around the south and east shoreline areas. Shoalwater substrates were mostly marl, muck, silt, and clay. Residential development is limited to several farm sites.

Yellow perch numbers were low in 2006 (2.65 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The yellow perch historical average catch rate is 7.43 fish/gillnet. The yellow perch average size was small (0.07 pounds and 5.60 inches from gillnets). Yellow perch growth rates were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-3. The 2004 year class comprised the majority of the 2006 yellow perch catch.

Northern pike numbers were moderate in 2006 (5.50 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The northern pike historical average catch rate is 5.53 fish/gillnet. The northern pike average size was moderate (3.34 pounds and 25.56 inches from gillnets). The northern pike historical average weight is 2.58 pounds from gillnets. The catch rate of quality size (21.00 inches) and larger northern pike was high (5.50 fish/gillnet) in the 2006 resurvey. Northern pike growth rates were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-6. Northern pike numbers captured in the 2006 resurvey comprised several different year classes.

Bluegill numbers were abundant in 2006 (96.00 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The bluegill historical average catch rate is 28.40 fish/trapnet. The 2006 bluegill average size was small (0.10 pounds and 5.03 inches from trapnets). The bluegill historical average weight is 0.15 pounds from trapnets. The catch rate of quality size (6.00 inches) and larger bluegill was high (22.33 fish/trapnet) in the 2006 resurvey. Bluegill growth rates were within or above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-6.

Black crappie numbers were abundant in 2006 (21.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The 2006 black crappie average size was small (0.12 pounds and 5.94 inches from gillnets). The black crappie historical average weight is 0.11 pounds from gillnets. The catch rate of quality size (8.00 inches) and larger black crappie was low (1.50 fish/gillnet) in the 2006 resurvey. Black crappie growth rates were generally above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-3. The 2005 and 2003 year classes comprised the majority of the 2006 black crappie catch.

Largemouth bass numbers were moderate to high in 2006 (1.50 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The largemouth bass average weight was moderate (1.37 pounds) in the 2006 gillnets. Largemouth bass growth rates were above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-6.

Walleye numbers were moderate in 2006 (7.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The walleye historical average catch rate is 7.27 fish/gillnet. The 2006 walleye average size was moderate (1.31 pounds and 14.75 inches from gillnets). The walleye historical average weight is 2.28 pounds. Walleye growth rates were within the Spicer Area normal range for ages 1-5. The catch rate of quality size (15.00 inches) and larger walleye was low (1.50 fish/gillnet) in 2006. The 2004 year class comprised the majority of the 2006 walleye catch. Walleye natural reproduction in Ella is generally insignificant or rare based on previous fall and summer surveys. Ella was stocked with 72,558 and 68,056 walleye fry in the spring of 2004 and 2005.

Black bullhead numbers were moderate in 2006 (40.50 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The black bullhead historical average catch rate is 30.03 fish/gillnet. The 2006 black bullhead average size was moderate (0.55 pounds and 9.95 inches from gillnets). The black bullhead historical average weight is 0.40 pounds from gillnets.

Carp numbers were moderate in 2006 (4.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The carp historical average catch rate is 1.93 fish/gillnet. The 2006 carp average size was moderate (4.27 pounds and 21.33 inches from gillnets). The historical average weight is 3.66 pounds from gillnets.

Current fish management activities on Ella include monitoring winter dissolved oxygen levels, protecting aquatic vegetation through the permit process, and stocking walleye fry every other year. The Ella fishery will be surveyed in the 2011 summer for all fish species. Fall electrofishing surveys will be conducted during walleye fry stocked years to assess the success or failure of the stockings. ?
