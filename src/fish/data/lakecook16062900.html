Status of the Fishery (as of 06/30/2003)

The walleye catch in 2003 was similar to catches observed in this lake since 1988. Walleye catches have generally not been high in this lake, but the lake is known to support a fair walleye fishery. Although the 2003 catch was dominated by a strong 2001 year class, a fair number of older, larger fish were also present. Growth of larger walleye had been average or better, and all walleye collected in 2003 had been produced naturally.

The lake trout catch was low compared to catches observed in this lake in the 1980's, and it was near the low end of the normal range for lakes of this type. Several lake trout year classes were included in the catch, all of which had been produced naturally. Lake trout growth had been faster than average for lakes of this type.

The northern pike catch was similar to catches observed in this lake in the past. Although northern pike numbers have not been high, the lake does at times produce some trophy-sized fish. The smallmouth bass catch in this lake has always been low, and that was true again in 2003. Smallmouth bass are not reliably sampled in gill nets, and it is likely that they are more abundant in this lake than netting data would indicate.

Forage for game fish was provided by a fairly dense population of 'dwarf' cisco. These fish, mostly northern cisco, were too small to be effectively sampled in standard gill nets in 2003; however, fairly high numbers of 4-5-inch fish were taken in small-mesh gill nets.

Anglers are reminded that most of Sea Gull Lake is inside the BWCAW. Most of the lake was closed to motorized use in 1999, including the popular walleye fishing area off the mouth of the Alpine River. Anglers visiting the lake should check with the USFS to obtain a map showing areas open for such use.
