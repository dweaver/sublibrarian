Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 06/30/2008)

A resurvey of Calhoun Lake was conducted in July of 2008. Calhoun is a moderately sized (618 acres) and moderately productive lake located in Kandiyohi County. The maximum depth is 13 feet. Residential development (51 homes/cabins & 5 recreational vehicles) has occurred primarily along the north and east shoreline areas. The immediate watershed is composed of agricultural row crop areas, hardwood tree stands, wetlands, grasslands, and residential homes. Agricultural row crop drainage into the lake occurs via several inlets/tiles/ditches located along the north, east, and west shore areas. The Middle Fork of Crow River enters Calhoun along the southwest corner. Calhoun is connected to Green Lake via a bypass channel of the Middle Fork Crow River and the Green Lake outlet marsh. A dam is located at the outlet on the south shore near the public access. Another dam is located on the Middle Fork of Crow River downstream of the bypass channel that flows into Calhoun. Water levels were normal during the 2008 spring and early summer, but low by fall. A DNR public access is located along the south shore. Large emergent vegetation (bulrush, cattails) stands were present along the west and south portions of the lake. Water quality (total phosphorus=0.033 mg/l) and clarity (secchi=6.0 feet) results were moderate during July of 2008. Submergent vegetation densities (northern milfoil, muskgrass, filamentous algae, water moss, various pondweed species) were moderate in 2008. Shoalwater substrates consisted mostly of sand, silt, and gravel. Calhoun is a popular bluegill and northern pike fishery. A partial (minimal) summerkill of bluegill (30-40 fish) and northern pike (1) due probably to columnaris was reported in June of 2007. Calhoun is managed primarily for largemouth bass, bluegill, black crappie, and northern pike.

Largemouth bass numbers were abundant in 2008 (5.33 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for Calhoun (25th=2.03, 50th=2.33, 75th=2.90 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The Calhoun largemouth bass historical average catch rate was 2.62 fish/gillnet. The 2008 largemouth bass average size was moderate (1.22 pounds and 12.80 inches) from gillnets. Largemouth bass growth rates were generally below the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-8. The 2002 year class comprised 33% of the 2008 total largemouth bass gillnet and trapnet catch. Abundant numbers of young of year "YOY" largemouth bass (55.75 fish/haul, 1.97 inches average length) were captured in the 2008 shoreline seine hauls.

Black crappie numbers were abundant in 2008 (14.67 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for Calhoun (25th=1.24, 50th=2.83, 75th=5.67 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The black crappie historical average gillnet catch was 4.68 fish/gillnet for Calhoun. The 2008 black crappie trapnet catch (8.14 fish/trapnet) was also above the Calhoun historical trapnet average (2.27 fish/trapnet). The 2008 black crappie average size was small (0.16 pounds and 6.36 inches) from gillnets, but moderate (0.33 pounds and 8.69 inches) from trapnets. The black crappie historical average weights were 0.31 pounds and 0.18 pounds from gillnets and trapnets respectively. The 2008 catch rate of preferred size (10 inches) and larger black crappie was low to moderate (1.60 fish/trapnet). Black crappie growth rates were below the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-4. The 2003 and 2002 year classes comprised 32% and 26% respectively of the total black crappie captured in the 2008 nets. Low numbers of YOY black crappie (2.00 fish/haul, 2.01 inches average length) were captured in the 2008 shoreline seine hauls.

Northern pike numbers were abundant in 2008 (14.33 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for Calhoun (25th=8.73, 50th=12.40, 75th=14.83 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The northern pike historical average catch rate is 12.86 fish/gillnet for Calhoun. The 2008 northern pike average size was small to moderate (2.72 pounds and 22.06 inches) from gillnets. The northern pike historical average weight is 2.56 pounds from gillnets. The 2008 catch rate of quality size (21.00 inches) and larger northern pike was high (8.33 fish/gillnet). Northern pike growth rates were generally within the Spicer Area normal ranges. Approximately 47% of the northern pike captured in the 2008 summer resurvey nets were from the 2006 year class.

Yellow perch were low in 2008 (3.00 fish/gillnet), but within the normal ranges for Calhoun (25th=2.38, 50th=8.13, 75th=13.03 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The yellow perch historical average catch rate is 10.31 fish/gillnet for Calhoun. The 2008 yellow perch average size was moderate (0.17 pounds and 7.10 inches) from gillnets. The yellow perch historical average weight is 0.15 pounds from gillnets. The catch rate of quality size (8.00 inches) and larger yellow perch was low (0.33 fish/gillnet) in 2008. Yellow perch growth rates were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-5. The 2004 year class comprised 50% of the 2008 yellow perch catch from gillnets and trapnets. Abundant numbers of YOY yellow perch (33.25 fish/haul, 2.22 inches average length) were captured in the 2008 shoreline seine hauls.

Walleye numbers were abundant in 2008 (4.67 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for Calhoun (25th=0.31, 50th=0.78, 75th=1.73 fish/gillnet) and within the normal range for similar lakes. The walleye historical catch rate is 1.42 fish/gillnet for Calhoun. The 2008 walleye average size was moderate (1.79 pounds and 16.39 inches) from gillnets. The walleye historical average weight is 2.05 pounds from gillnets. The catch rate of quality size (15.00 inches) and larger walleye was moderate (3.67 fish/gillnet) in 2008. Walleye growth rates were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-6. The 2005 year class (stocked as yearlings in 2006) comprised 69% of the 2008 walleye catch from gillnets and trapnets. Walleye yearlings and adults were stocked in Calhoun during 2004 (9,865 fish, 655 pounds), 2006 (728 fish, 468 pounds), and 2007 (1,557 fish, 150 pounds).

Bluegill were moderate to abundant in 2008 (49.14 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal ranges for Calhoun (25th=19.14, 50th=38.18, 75th=65.03 fish/trapnet and similar lakes. The bluegill historical catch rate is 60.80 fish/trapnet for Calhoun. The 2008 bluegill average size was small to moderate (0.13 pounds and 5.54 inches) from trapnets. The bluegill historical average weight is 0.11 pounds from trapnets. The catch rate of quality size (6.00 inches) and larger bluegill was moderate (15.38 fish/trapnet) in 2008. Bluegill growth rates in Calhoun Lake were below the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-6, but within the normal ranges for ages 7-8. The 2002 year class comprised 49% of the bluegill catch in the 2008 nets. Abundant numbers of YOY bluegill (43.25 fish/haul, 1.19 inches average length) were captured in 2008 shoreline seine hauls. Approximately 1,316 pounds of small (< 6 inches) adult bluegill (9,212 fish) were removed during the spring of 2007 from Calhoun for stocking into other area lakes with inadequate bluegill natural reproduction.

Black bullhead were low in 2008 (9.33 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for Calhoun (25th=9.31, 50th=19.46, 75th=56.70 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes (25th=30.34 fish/gillnet). The 2008 black bullhead trapnet catch was also low (0.29 fish/trapnet). The 2008 black bullhead average size was large (0.70 pounds and 10.39 inches) from gillnets. The black bullhead historical average weight is 0.41 pounds from gillnets.

Other fish species of special interest captured in the 2008 gillnets included moderate numbers of carp (2.00 fish/gillnet), low numbers of pumpkinseed sunfish (2.14 fish/trapnet), and high numbers of yellow bullhead (32.67 fish/gillnet). The Calhoun historical average catch rates were 0.55 carp/gillnet, 15.88 pumpkinseed sunfish/trapnet, and 16.60 yellow bullhead/gillnet. The 2008 average weights were 2.30 pounds, 0.13 pounds, and 0.50 pounds for carp, pumpkinseed sunfish, and yellow bullhead respectively.

Current fish management activities on Calhoun Lake include monitoring the fish population on a periodic basis, protecting aquatic vegetation through the permit process, operating the outlet dam to prevent winterkill, and stocking various fish species as warranted. The Calhoun fishery will be surveyed in the 2013 spring and summer for all fish species. ?
