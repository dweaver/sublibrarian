* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Fish Consumption Guidelines

No fish consumption guidelines are available for this lake. For more information, see the "Fish Consumption Advice" pages at the Minnesota Department of Health.


Status of the Fishery (as of 07/11/2007)

A fish population assessment was conducted during mid July of 2007 in Tyson Lake. Tyson is a small (147 acres), shallow (8 feet maximum depth), periodically aerated, and productive lake located in Yellow Medicine County. Residential development is limited to one farm site on the south shore. Nutrient runoff into Tyson is from agricultural row crops. Tyson Lake is connected to a shallow wetland along the east shore. Water clarity was poor during July of 2007 (0.5 feet). Blue-green algae blooms are common and intense in Tyson Lake. A partial summerkill of primarily black bullhead, fathead minnows, and orangespotted sunfish occurred in July of 2002. Submergent vegetation is limited to primarily sago pondweed, water moss, and filamentous algae, especially in the near shore areas. Emergent vegetation consists mainly of bulrush and cattail species scattered throughout the shoreline areas. Tyson Lake is managed for walleye as primary species with yellow perch and bluegill as secondary species. Walleye fry are generally stocked into Tyson every other year. If a fry stocking is a failure, then the lake will generally be stocked again with fry the following spring. Walleye fingerling stocking is contingent upon two successive walleye fry stocking failures in Tyson Lake.

Yellow perch numbers were low in 2007 (1.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, and below the Tyson historical average (5.53 fish/gillnet). The 2007 yellow perch average size was small (0.11 pounds and 5.91 inches) from gillnets. The yellow perch historical average weight is 0.42 pounds from gillnets. Yellow perch growth rates in Tyson were above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-2. The 2005 yellow perch year class comprised 92% of the 2007 yellow perch assessment catch.

Bluegill numbers were low in 2007 (0.11 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, and below the Tyson historical average (3.44 fish/trapnet). Adult bluegill (2,037 fish, 291 pounds) were stocked in Tyson during July of 2004.

Green sunfish numbers were low to moderate in 2007 (0.22 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, and below the Tyson historical average (1.04 fish/trapnet). The 2007 green sunfish average size was large (0.22 pounds and 6.30 inches) from trapnets. Orangespotted sunfish numbers were low in 2007 (0.11 fish/trapnet) and below the Tyson historical average (6.24 fish/trapnet).

Walleye numbers were low to moderate in the 2007 gillnets (4.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. However, walleye numbers were high in the 2007 trapnets (9.67 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The walleye historical average catch rates are 1.77 fish/gillnet and 2.04 fish/trapnet for Tyson. The 2007 walleye average size was small from gillnets (0.76 pounds and 11.52 inches) and trapnets (0.41 pounds and 9.85 inches). The walleye historical average weights are 1.77 pounds from gillnets and 1.70 pounds from trapnets. Walleye growth rates for Tyson were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-2, and above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 3-5. The 2005 walleye year class comprised 98% of the 2007 walleye catch. Walleye natural reproduction in Tyson is generally infrequent and insignificant based on previous fall and summer surveys.

A fall electrofishing survey was conducted to evaluate a 2007 spring walleye fry stocking (114,545 fry). There were no young of year "YOY" walleye captured in the 2007 fall electrofishing survey. There were moderate YOY walleye numbers (54.00 YOY/hour, 6.49 inches average length) captured in 2005. The Tyson Lake fall electrofishing historical average catch rate and length are 63.60 YOY/hour and 6.25 inches respectively. The Spicer Area YOY walleye historical average catch rate is 71.24 YOY/hour from lakes fry stocked and lakes with natural reproduction. Walleye fry have been stocked into Tyson during 1998, 2000, 2002, 2003, 2005, and 2007. The highest Tyson YOY walleye catch rate occurred during a 1998 fry stocked year (264.00 YOY/hour, 6.01 inches average length).

Black bullhead numbers were abundant in 2007 (298.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, and above the historical average (187.43 fish/gillnet). The 2007 black bullhead average size was small to moderate (0.18 pounds and 7.11 inches) from gillnets. The black bullhead historical average weight is 0.32 pounds from gillnets.

Current fish management activities on Tyson include monitoring the fish population on a periodic basis, monitoring winter dissolved oxygen levels, assisting the Wood Lake Sportsmans Club with the operation of the aeration system, protecting aquatic vegetation through the permit process, participating in local watershed initiatives, and stocking various fish species as warranted. The Tyson fishery will be sampled in the 2008 fall for YOY walleye. ?
