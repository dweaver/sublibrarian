* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 08/11/2008)

Lake Le Homme Dieu is a 1,744-acre basin located on the northeast edge of Alexandria. It is part of the Alexandria Chain of Lakes and connected to Lake Carlos and Lake Geneva via navigable channels. Residential development surrounds the entire lake. Water quality and clarity are considered good. Secchi disk transparency averages about 9.0 feet during summer months. Because of it's clear water and close proximity to Alexandria, this lake is a very popular destination for both anglers and recreational boaters. Boating pressure can be extremely high, especially on weekend days during summer months. Because of its popularity, two relatively large public accesses are present. Rotary Beach access is located on the northwest shoreline off County Road 42. Krueger's Creek access is located along the northeast shoreline. The City of Alexandria maintains a public swimming beach on the southeast shoreline. Another popular beach area is located along the northwest shoreline adjacent to the connection to Lake Carlos. Boaters should be cautious of marked navigation hazards on Lake Le Homme Dieu. A shallow rock bar is located in the middle of the lake off the west shoreline. Shallow rock areas are also present off both points leading to the west bay. Two protected fish spawning areas are posted on Lake Le Homme Dieu. Fishing in these areas is prohibited from April 1st through June 30th.

Lake Le Homme Dieu supports a well-balanced fish community and provides diverse angling opportunities. The 2008 lake survey revealed a fish community typical of many bass-panfish lakes found throughout west-central Minnesota. Largemouth bass are abundant and provide a very popular fishery. In fact, the Alexandria Chain of Lakes is host to several bass tournaments throughout the year. Although rare, bass anglers might also encounter smallmouth bass. This species was first sampled in 2000 and continued presence is likely due to migration from connected basins. Panfishing is also popular during both open water and ice fishing seasons. Anglers can expect good numbers of both black crappie and bluegill. Larger crappie are relatively common, whereas most bluegill tend to be small. Harvestable-size yellow perch are also present but abundance is low. Northern pike are abundant. Larger pike are present but "hammer handles" (i.e., small pike) comprise the majority of this population. Walleye anglers can expect low to moderate numbers of harvestable-size fish. Average size of 2008 gill net captures was 15.9 inches and 1.7 pounds.

Other species sampled in the 2008 survey included the following: black, brown, and yellow bullhead, bowfin (dogfish), common carp, hybrid sunfish, pumpkinseed, rock bass, shorthead redhorse, tullibee (cisco), and white sucker.

Anglers can play an important role in improving fish community and predator/prey balance. There are excess numbers of small largemouth bass, northern pike, and bluegill. Harvest is necessary to reduce competition for food and increase gamefish productivity. ?
