Status of the Fishery (as of 08/23/1999)

Big Wolf Lake, in southern Beltrami County is connected by the Mississippi River to the Cass Lake chain, and fish populations are influenced by movement between the lakes. Primary fish species in Big Wolf Lake are walleye, nothern pike, and yellow perch. All of the species making up the fish community are maintained through natural reproduction. Care is taken to protect the in-lake habitat to ensure continued successful reproduction.

Walleye were sampled in 1999 at an all time high for this lake. Most walleye sampled were between 12.0 and 16.0 inches, and average length was 13.9 inches. Average weight has remained constant at approximately one pound through the entire assessment history, back to 1948. The largest walleye sampled was 24.6 inches. Some walleye have black spot, a common external parasite of fish. Fish with heavy infestations are not aesthetically pleasing but eating quality is not affected.

Northern pike were also sampled at their highest recorded abundance over all assessments. Average length and weight were 20.8 inches and 2.3 pounds respectively. Aging suggests that successful natural reproduction occurs most years. The largest nothern pike sampled was 33 inches.

Yellow perch catch rate has remained relatively stable through all assessments, but abundance is not high enough for Big Wolf Lake to be known as one of the "hot" perch lakes. Average size has decreased over time but with a mean and maximum perch length of 6.6 and 9.9 inches respectively, many will still be acceptable to anglers. Black spot was noted on 33% of the perch examined and yellow grub (another unharmful external parasite) was also present.

Tullibee catch rate has shifted up and down over the years with no apparent trend. An average weight of 1.1 pounds is comparable with most other assessments. About half of the tullibee sampled had an internal parasite (Triaenophorus) which may be transmitted to humans. All fish havested should be thoroughly cooked to prevent possible transmission of parasites to humans.

Other species sampled in small numbers in the 1999 assessment were white sucker, black crappie, rock bass, shorthead redhorse, and yellow bullheads.

Muskellunge have never been sampled in an assessment in Big Wolf Lake but they are known to be present.
