Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 08/22/2005)

Prairie is a 848 acre lake with 272 (32%) acres of littoral area and a maximum depth of 47 feet. The lake is located seven miles north of Cromwell, MN and has a DNR administered, concrete, back-in access located off State Highway #73. Walleye fry have been stocked in even years since 1998 and every third year from 1986 through 1998. Northern pike have been managed since 2003 with a minimum length for harvest of 30 inches. Walleye abundance of 1.7 per lift was below average compared to other Minnesota lakes of similar type. Walleye abundance, which had increased slightly compared to the 2000 investigation (1.6), has declined from the 1990 and 1995 catch rates of 5.3 and 4.0 per gillnet lift. Mean length of 13.3 inches was average. Growth rates had improved considerably since 2000 but were still slow compared to other Duluth Area populations. Of the walleye aged from the 2005 investigation, 70% were born in years that walleye fry were stocked. Similarly, 63% of walleye aged in 2000 were born in years that walleye fry were stocked. A spring electrofishing assessment of spawning walleye was conducted in 2005. A total of 151 walleye were captured for a catch rate of 83.8 per hour. Mean length was 13.8 inches and growth rates were slow. Age analysis revealed strong 2000 and 2001 year-classes and all year-classes from 1997 through 2004 represented. Largemouth bass were sampled by electrofishing in the spring of 2005. No largemouth bass were sampled during this investigation. However, one young-of-the-year largemouth bass was documented from shoreline seining during the summer investigation. Northern pike abundance of 1.8 per gillnet lift was slightly below average compared to other Minnesota lakes of similar type. The percentage of northern pike longer than 21 inches had declined from 55 in 2000 to 48 in 2005. However, the percentage of northerns longer than 28 inches had increased from 0 in 1995 to 5 and 19 in 2000 and 2005. Growth rates had improved from average to fast from 1995 to 2005 compared to other Duluth Area populations. Age analysis revealed a strong 2002 year-class and five year-classes represented from 1998 through 2003. Yellow perch abundance of 6.0 per gillnet lift was average compared to other Minnesota lakes of similar type. Mean length of 6.4 inches was small and growth was average. Age analysis revealed strong 2002 and 2003 year-classes and no fish older than age five. Tullibee abundance of 2.3 per gillnet lift was average compared to other Minnesota lakes of similar type. Tullibee mean length was 12.4 inches and growth rates had declined slightly since 1995. Age analysis revealed fairly steady recruitment from 1997 through 2002. Black crappie abundance of 1.3 per trapnet lift was average compared to other Minnesota lakes of similar type. Mean length of 9.5 inches was large and growth was average. Age analysis revealed a strong 2001 year-class and all year-classes from 1999 through 2003 represented. Bluegill abundance of 1.8 per lift was below average compared to other Minnesota lakes of similar type. Mean length of 7.1 inches was large and growth was fast. Age analysis revealed a strong 2001 year-class and all year-classes from 1999 through 2003 represented. Other fish species sampled include blacknose shiner, bowfin, brown bullhead, Iowa darter, logperch, rock bass, shorthead redhorse, white sucker and yellow bullhead. In summary, although walleye catch rates have been relatively low from the last two investigations, fry stocking continues to provide significant contributions to the population. It also appears that the northern pike regulation may initially be resulting in larger pike in the population.
