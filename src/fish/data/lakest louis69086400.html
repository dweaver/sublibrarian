Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 06/16/2008)

Ash is a class 5 lake located about 10 miles north of Orr, MN. Northern pike, panfish, smallmouth bass and yellow perch represent the majority of the sport-fish community. Early investigations of Ash Lake indicated an unbalanced fish community with low predator abundance and extremely high forage abundance. Efforts to reshape the community have included centrarchid and walleye stocking since 1909; white sucker and yellow perch removal from 1980-1985; and starting on May 10, 2003, the use of a special regulation to protect northern pike from 24-36 inches from angler harvest. Efforts to reshape the fish community have provided some very positive benefits. The 2008 assessment was conducted to collect information to be used with future assessments to evaluate the special regulation on northern pike, walleye stocking, and community balance.

Black crappie trap-net abundance was 7.83 fish/set, which was well above the level found in similar lakes. In 2008, four year-classes were present and age ranged from 3-6 years. The 2005 year-class represented 85% of the sample. Total length in trap nets ranged from 6.3-11.3 inches and the mean was 7.9.

Since the northern pike regulation was established in 2003 on Ash Lake, density has decreased and growth rate and size have increased. Northern pike gill-net abundance (4.33 fish/set) decreased from the level observed in 2002 (5.33 fish/set). Total length at age 4 was 26.3 inches in 2008 compared to 22.9 inches in 2002 and much greater than the statewide median of 20.5 inches. In 2008, nine year-classes were present and age ranged from 1-9 years. The 2004 year-class represented 30% of the sample. Total length ranged from 15.9-35.8 inches and the mean was 28.5; the mean in 2002 was 22.5 inches. The mean weight of northern pike captured in gill nets has increased from 2.88 pounds in 2002 to 5.90 pounds in 2008.

Night electrofishing was used to sample a total of 239 smallmouth bass on one sampling night in early June. Smallmouth bass were captured at a rate of 179.1 per hour of on-time, which was the highest level ever observed in Ash Lake, but similar to the 1998 investigation (170.5 per hour of on-time). Total length ranged from 3.6 to 17.7 inches and the mean was 8.7. Age ranged from 1 to 10 and the 2005 year-class represented 62% of the sample.

Walleye gill-net abundance was 0.78 fish/set, which was well below what is found in similar lakes and quite disappointing considering the stocking rate was increased to three pounds per littoral acre in 2003. Four year-classes were present (two were from stocked years). Age ranged from 3-10 years. Total length ranged from 10.9-28.5 inches and the mean was 19.6.

Yellow perch gill-net abundance was 97.56 fish/set, which was well above the third quartile of 14.13 for similar lakes. The abundance in 2008 was a noticeable increase from the 2005 investigation (11.22 fish/set), but similar to the 2002 investigation (107.22 fish/set) in which abundance was approaching levels observed in the late 1970s and early 1980s that prompted a fish removal project. In 2008, five year-classes were present and age ranged from 2-6 years. The 2005 year-class represented 77% of the sample. Total length ranged from 5.4-10.2 inches and the mean was 7.3. ?
