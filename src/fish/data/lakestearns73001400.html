Status of the Fishery (as of 06/23/2005)

Lake Marie is a 147 acre lake located along the border of Wright and Stearns Counties, 2 miles northwest of South Haven. The public access is located on the northeast side of the lake and is the property of the Stearns County Parks Department. Lake Marie and connecting Lake Louisa are reservoir lakes of the Clearwater River and were formed by a dam on the outlet of Lake Marie. In 2005, all the lakes along the Clearwater River were sampled including: Louisa, Marie, Caroline, Augusta, Clearwater, and Grass lakes.

Based on the survey anglers can expect good fishing for largemouth bass and above average size sunfish. Angling for northern pike will produce fair fishing for above average size fish.

Largemouth Bass Electrofishing Survey Largemouth bass electrofishing on Lake Marie was combined with connecting Louisa Lake. A total of 95 largemouth bass were sampled in 1.05 hours of electrofishing. The largemouth bass catch rate was 90.3 fish/hour, over one and a half times the average catch rate for Montrose area lakes (52/hr). Largemouth bass ranged in length from 3.5 to 20.5 inches with a mean length of 12.3 inches.

The northern pike gill net catch rate (2.8 fish/net) was the same as that observed in 1997. Fourteen northern pike were sampled. Fish ranged in length from 18.6 to 39.4 inches. Mean length and weight of northern pike were 28.6 inches and 5.8 pounds. Few lakes in the area have such quality northern pike.

Only two walleye were sampled during the gill net survey. The low catch of walleye is not surprising since Lake Marie is not stocked. The walleye in the lake have probably migrated from upstream lakes.

Four channel catfish were sampled for the first time in Lake Marie. These fish probably migrated downstream from Betsy Lake which was first stocked with channel catfish in 1983.

The bluegill trap net catch rate (23.7 fish/net) appeared lower than that observed in 1997 (48.0 fish/net) but was above the average for lakes similar to Marie. Bluegill ranged in length from 3.4 inches to 8.5 inches with a mean length of 6.9 inches, considered good.

The black crappie trap net catch rate (9.9 fish/net) was lower than that of 1997 (26.3 fish/net) but above the average rate for lakes similar to Marie. A total of 89 black crappie were sampled by trap net. Fish ranged in length from 3.5 to 10.0 inches with a mean length of 8.2 inches.

Other fish species sampled during the survey included black and yellow bullhead, bowfin, hybrid and pumpkinseed sunfish, golden shiner and white sucker.
