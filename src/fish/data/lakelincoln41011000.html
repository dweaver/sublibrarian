* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 07/02/2008)

Hendricks is a 1,600 acre, class 41 lake located on the Minnesota-South Dakota border in the town of Hendricks, Minnesota. The Minnesota Department of Natural Resources and South Dakota Department of Game, Fish, and Parks cooperatively manage the lake for walleye, perch, and northern pike.

Overall fish abundance in Hendricks Lake was low. Walleye abundance was the lowest ever recorded for Hendricks Lake. Fish averaged 16.3 inches long and 1.7 lbs. Additional walleye stocking is ongoing at Hendricks Lake to boost the walleye population, but anglers should find walleye fishing difficult on Hendricks Lake. Northern pike abundance has increased from the historical low observed in 2005. Pike averaged 22.4 inches long and 2.6 lbs. Pike should provide some fishing for anglers over the next few years. Yellow perch abundance has appeared to decrease from 2005. Similarly, the average size of yellow perch also declined. In 2005 perch averaged 10.5 inches long, whereas perch in 2008 only averaged 6.7 inches long. This small average size is likely due to the abundance of perch from the 2007 year class, which made up 68% of perch aged. Anglers may find some success fishing for perch, but average size will likely be small. White bass abundance has declined slightly since 2005. However, size was large, with the white bass sampled in 2008 being 15-16 inches long and seven years old (2002 year class). Although not abundant, white bass of quality size can be caught in Hendricks Lake. Additional game fish found in low abundance were black crappie, bluegill, and hybrid sunfish. Non-game fish sampled were black bullhead, common carp, and white sucker. Future management activities include fish stocking, monitoring of winter dissolved oxygen levels and fishing pressure, and evaluations of YOY fish populations.?
