Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 07/15/2004)

A fish population assessment was conducted during mid July on Willmar Lake. Willmar is a moderate sized (435 acres), shallow (14 feet maximum depth), aerated, and productive lake located in Kandiyohi County. Willmar Lake is located within the city limits of Willmar. A large golf course borders the north shore area. A city park is located along the south shore. Residential development occurs primarily along the west shore. A major four-lane highway abuts along the east shore. Willmar Lake is connected to Foot Lake via an outlet channel along the southwest portion adjacent to the city park. Willmar Lake is also connected to several shallow lakes and Eagle Lake via inlets along the north shore. Water clarity is often stained (coffee colored) throughout the summer months. Blue-green algae blooms are common on Willmar Lake. Aquatic vegetation is limited to primarily sago pondweed in the lake proper and cattails along the south portion of the lake. Willmar Lake is managed primarily for walleye, channel catfish, and black crappie. Walleye fry are generally stocked into Willmar every other year. If a fry stocking is a failure, then the lake will generally be stocked again with fry the following spring. Walleye fingerling stocking is contingent upon two successive walleye fry stocking failures in Willmar Lake.

Channel catfish numbers were low in 2004 (0.33 fish/gillnet), but similar to the Willmar Lake historical average (0.53 fish/gillnet). The 2004 channel catfish average size was moderate (4.29 pounds) from gillnets and trapnets combined in Willmar Lake. The largest channel catfish captured in the 2004 assessment was 25.20 inches. Channel catfish were first stocked into Willmar Lake during 1994 (2,180 yearlings). Channel catfish were recently stocked in 1996 (6,564 yearlings), 1998 (6,665 yearlings), 2001 (3,431 fingerlings), and 2004 (6,524 fingerlings). Channel catfish stockings are currently proposed for a three-year rotation with the next scheduled stocking for 2005 in Willmar Lake.

Yellow perch numbers were moderate in 2004 (8.67 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The yellow perch historical average catch rate is 2.47 fish/gillnet for Willmar. The 2004 yellow perch average size was moderate (0.17 pounds and 6.95 inches) from gillnets. The yellow perch historical average weight is 0.18 pounds from gillnets.

Northern pike numbers were abundant in 2004 (7.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The northern pike historical catch rate is 4.33 fish/gillnet for Willmar. The 2004 northern pike average size was small (2.15 pounds and 20.85 inches) from gillnets. The northern pike historical average weight is 3.84 pounds from gillnets. The largest northern pike captured in the 2004 assessment was 32.01 inches. The catch rate of northern pike 21.00 inches and larger was moderate (4.00 fish/gillnet) in the 2004 assessment. Northern pike growth rates for Willmar were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-3. The 2001 northern pike year class comprised 45% of the 2004 assessment northern pike catch.

Bluegill numbers were abundant in 2004 (20.83 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The bluegill historical catch rate is 5.40 fish/trapnet for Willmar. The 2004 bluegill average size was moderate (0.19 pounds and 5.95 inches) from trapnets. The bluegill historical average weight is 0.25 pounds from trapnets.

Black crappie numbers were abundant in 2004 (30.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The black crappie historical catch rate is 10.07 fish/gillnet for Willmar. The 2004 black crappie average size was small (0.14 pounds and 5.51 inches) from gillnets. The black crappie historical average weight is 0.29 pounds from gillnets. The catch rate of black crappie 8.00 inches and larger was moderate (5.33 fish/gillnet) in the 2004 assessment. Black crappie growth rates for Willmar were near the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-3. The 2003 black crappie year class comprised 65% of the 2004 black crappie assessment catch.

Walleye numbers were moderate in 2004 (6.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, but substantially below the walleye historical catch rate (16.53 fish/gillnet) for Willmar. The 2004 walleye average size was moderate (1.72 pounds and 16.87 inches) from gillnets. The walleye historical average weight is 1.18 pounds from gillnets. The largest walleye captured in the 2004 assessment was 21.18 inches. The catch rate of walleye 15.00 inches and larger was moderate (4.67 fish/gillnet) in 2004. Walleye growth rates for Willmar were variable compared to the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-7. The 2000 walleye year class (fry stocked year) comprised 38% of the 2004 walleye assessment catch. Walleye natural reproduction in Willmar is generally infrequent and insignificant based on previous fall and summer surveys.

A fall electrofishing survey was conducted to evaluate a 2004 spring walleye fry stocking (331,004 fry). There were low to moderate young of year "YOY" walleye numbers (34.00 YOY/hour, 6.14 inches average length) captured in 2004. Other area walleye fry stocked lakes had generally low YOY walleye catch rates in 2004 fall electrofishing surveys. The Willmar Lake fall electrofishing historical average catch rate and length are 155.67 YOY/hour and 5.73 inches respectively. Walleye fry have been stocked recently into Willmar during 1994, 1996, 1998, 2000, and 2002. The highest fall electrofishing catch rate of YOY walleye occurred in 1994 (528.00 YOY/hour, 4.58 inches average length) for a fry stocked year.

Black bullhead numbers were abundant in 2004 (102.67 fish/gillnet) and near the upper end of the normal range for similar lakes. The black bullhead historical average catch rate is 164.00 fish/gillnet for Willmar. The 2004 black bullhead average size is moderate to large (0.40 pounds and 8.69 inches) from gillnets. The black bullhead historical average weight is 0.33 pounds from gillnets.

Carp numbers were moderate in 2004 (3.50 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The carp historical average catch rate is 3.37 fish/trapnet for Willmar. The 2004 carp average size was moderate (4.21 pounds and 20.02 inches) from trapnets. The carp historical average weight is 4.69 pounds from trapnets.

Freshwater drum numbers were abundant in 2004 (7.67 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, but substantially below the freshwater drum historical average catch rate (16.33 fish/gillnet) for Willmar. The 2004 freshwater drum average size was moderate (1.28 pounds and 13.59 inches) from gillnets. The freshwater drum historical average weight is 0.74 pounds for gillnets.

Current fish management activities on Willmar include monitoring the fish population on a periodic basis, monitoring winter dissolved oxygen levels, assisting the county with the operation of the aeration system, protecting aquatic vegetation through the permit process, participating in local watershed initiatives, and stocking various fish species as warranted. The Willmar fishery will be sampled in the 2008 summer for all fish species.
