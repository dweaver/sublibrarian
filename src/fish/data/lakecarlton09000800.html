Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 08/30/2004)

Chub Lake is a 311 acre lake located near Carlton, Minnesota with 54% (169 acres) littoral area and a maximum depth of 28 feet. A state owned, concrete back-in access is located on the north end of the lake, near Chub Lake park. Walleye and largemouth bass are the primary fish species. Nearly 25,000 fingerling and 6,000 yearling walleyes have been stocked since 1990. Although walleye abundance has decreased some since the investigation in 1999, a walleye abundance of 3.3 fish/gillnet in 2004 was still above average compared to Minnesota lakes of similar type. Mean length was 21.6 inches with 95% of walleyes 15 inches or greater. Growth was slow compared to other Duluth Area populations. Correlation of fish to stocked years was 95% in 2004 and 65% of walleyes were correlated to the 1996 fingerling stocking. Largemouth bass electrofishing catch rates of 133 per hour were well above the average (32.6 per hour) for Duluth Area bass populations. Mean length was 12.6 inches with 68% percent of largemouth bass 12 inches or larger. Largemouth bass in Chub Lake successfully reproduced in all years between 1991 and 2002, but growth was slow compared to other Duluth Area populations. Bluegill abundance was slightly below average for Minnesota lakes with an average of 8.0 fish per trapnet. Mean length was 5.5 inches with 41% of bluegills 6 inches or greater and a maximum size of 6.9 inches. Bluegill reproduction was consistent; however, growth was slow compared to other Duluth Area populations. Northern pike abundance was average for Minnesota lakes with a value of 7.2 fish/gillnet. Mean length was 22.7 inches, with most (70%) northern pike larger than 20 inches. Growth was slow for Duluth Area populations. Based on age distribution, northern pike successfully reproduced from 1996 to 2001. Black crappie abundance was 0.9 fish/trapnet and was average for Minnesota lakes of this type. The mean length of black crappie was 7.4 inches, and growth was average for Duluth Area populations. Only three year classes were represented in 2004. Yellow perch abundance was average for Minnesota lakes with a value of 4.0 fish/gillnet. Mean length of yellow perch captured was 6.4 inches with a maximum length of 6.9 inches. Other fish species sampled included brown bullhead, pumpkinseed, rock bass, and white sucker. Largemouth bass catch rates in 2004 were among the highest observed in the Duluth Area. In order to protect spawning largemouth bass, fishing and boating in the southeast bay will continue to be prohibited from the opening of the general fishing season to June 30. Walleye catch rates in Chub Lake have dropped from a historic high of 24.0 fish/gillnet in 1989 to 3.3 fish/gillnet in 2004, despite the increased amount and frequency of walleye stocking between 1989 and 2002. The relatively high predator density (i.e., northern pike and largemouth bass) may limit the success of fingerling walleye stocking.
