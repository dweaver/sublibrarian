Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 08/06/2007)

A population assessment was conducted at Marion Lake in 2007. A drought occurred during the summer of 2007 resulting in low lake levels. An aerator is operated during the winter at Marion Lake and winterkill has not been reported for many years. Marion is primarily managed for bluegill, black crappie and walleye.

A total of 86 walleye were gill netted for a catch rate of 9.6/net, which indicated that good numbers of fish were present for this type of lake. The catch rate in 2005 was 12.8/net. In 2007 gill netted walleye were 8.5-26.8 inches long averaging 14.4 inches. Approximately 55% of the gill net catch was 13 inches or longer. Walleye were 1-12 years old with 12 year-classes present. Years when walleye were stocked accounted for 71% of the gill net catch, although some or all of that proportion could have come from natural reproduction. Age 2 fish made up 38% of the gill net catch in 2007. The current walleye stocking regime (1,000 fry/littoral acre two-out-of-three years) was initiated in 1995. Gill net catches during the current regime (2000,2003,2005,2007) ranged from 2.6-12.8/net, averaging 8.7/net.

A total of 21 northern pike were gill netted for a catch rate of 2.3/net, which indicated that low numbers were present. The catch rate in 2005 was 5.0/net. Gill net catch rates for northern pike have been decreasing since the 2000 assessment. In 2007, northern pike were 17.8-30.2 inches long averaging 22.5 inches. Trap nets sampled northern pike up to 32.3 inches in length. Northern pike were 1-6 with 6 year-classes present. Anglers reported a summerkill of northern pike in 2006, which may have been linked to abnormally high water temperatures.

A total of 27 yellow perch were gill netted for a catch rate of 3.0/net, which indicated that low numbers were present. The catch rate in 2005 was 55.9/net. In 2007, yellow perch were 5.5-8.7 inches long averaging 6.8 inches. Yellow perch were 2-4 years old with 3 year-classes present.

A total of 39 channel catfish were gill netted for a catch rate 4.3/net, which indicated that moderate numbers were present. The catch rate in 2005 was 1.0/net. In 2007, gill netted channel catfish were 11.0-30.9 inches long averaging 14.7 inches. Channel catfish were 2-12 years old with 7 year-classes present. Age 2 fish accounted for 78% of the fish that could be aged. Since fish were not stocked in 2005, this indicated that channel catfish were capable of producing large year-classes naturally. Only 14% of the age sample corresponded with stocked year-classes. The old stocking regime of 10 yearlings/littoral acre every-other-year began in 1992 and ended in 2002. A new regime (30 fingerlings/littoral acre every-other-year) began in 2004.

A total of 31 black crappie were trap netted for a catch rate of 3.4/net, which is typical for this type of lake. The trap net catch rate in 2003 was 4.2/net. Trap net catch rates for black crappie have been decreasing since the 1995 assessment. In 2007, trap netted black crappie were 5.0-10.0 inches long averaging 7.8 inches. A total of 286 black crappie were gill netted for a catch rate of 31.8/net, indicating that high numbers were present. The gill net catch rate in 2005 was 9.9/net. Gill net catch rates of black crappie have been up and down since the 1995 assessment, showing no obvious trend. In 2007, gill netted black crappie were 3.6-10.8 inches long averaging 7.6 inches. Approximately 35% of the gill net catch was 8 inches or longer. Black crappie were 1-5 years old with 5 year-classes present. Age 2 fish accounted for 73% of the gill net catch.

A total of 10 bluegill were trap netted for a catch rate of 1.1/net, which indicated that low numbers were present. The 2007 catch rate was the lowest on record since the lake was reclaimed with rotenone in 1979. The catch rate in 2003 was 17.7/net. Bluegill catch rates have been decreasing since the 1996 assessment. In 2007, bluegill were 2.8-7.0 inches long averaging 5.5 inches. Bluegill were 1-4 years old with 4 year-classes present.

A total of 5 carp were trap netted for a catch rate of 0.6/net, which indicated that low numbers were present. The catch rate in 2003 was 0.9/net. In 2007, trap netted carp were 18.6-26.0 inches long averaging 22.7 inches.

Black bullhead were not sampled in gill nets or trap nets in 2007. Trap net catch rates have been low at Marion Lake since the 1981 assessment.

A total of 13 largemouth bass were captured during spring night-time electrofishing, for a catch rate of 9.2/hour, indicating that a fair sized population was present. The catch rate in 2003 was 17.7/hour. In 2007, electrofished largemouth bass were 8.5-18.7 inches long averaging 12.8 inches. Trap nets sampled 4 largemouth bass up to 19.1 inches in length. Largemouth bass were 2-7 years old with 5 year-classes present. ?
