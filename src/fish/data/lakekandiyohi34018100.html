Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Fish Consumption Guidelines

No fish consumption guidelines are available for this lake. For more information, see the "Fish Consumption Advice" pages at the Minnesota Department of Health.


Status of the Fishery (as of 07/19/2004)

A fish population assessment was conducted on Foot Lake during mid July of 2004. Foot is a moderate sized (694 acres), multi basin, aerated, and productive lake located in Kandiyohi County. Foot Lake is located within the city limits of Willmar. A large city park is located along the east side of the lake. There are two distinct basins (northwest and southeast) bisected by a road, but connected via several culverts. Foot Lake is also connected to Willmar Lake via an upstream shallow channel. Foot Lake has two public access sites with one site on each basin. The northwest basin is 6 feet deep maximum with abundant submergent vegetation (coontail, northern milfoil) and moderate water clarity. The southeast basin has a maximum depth of 24 feet with sparse submergent vegetation (sago pondweed) and poor water clarity (secchi=3.0 feet). Blue-green algae blooms are common occurrences during the late summer months on Foot Lake. The Foot Lake outlet is considered the headwaters of Hawk Creek, although there are several shallow lakes upstream connected via ditches and inlets to Willmar Lake. Foot Lake has been a popular fishing lake for black crappie, walleye, northern pike, bluegill, and largemouth bass in recent years. Walleye fry are generally stocked into Foot every other year. If a fry stocking is a failure, then the lake will generally be stocked again with fry the following spring. Walleye fingerling stocking is contingent upon two successive walleye fry stocking failures in Foot Lake.

Yellow perch numbers were moderate in 2004 (14.67 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The yellow perch historical average catch rate is 4.93 fish/gillnet for Foot Lake. The 2004 yellow perch average size was moderate (0.18 pounds and 7.13 inches) from gillnets. The yellow perch historical average weight is 0.19 pounds from gillnets.

Northern pike numbers were abundant in 2004 (10.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The northern pike historical catch rate is 3.20 fish/gillnet for Foot Lake. The 2004 northern pike average size was moderate (2.45 pounds and 22.01 inches) from gillnets. The northern pike historical average weight is 2.51 pounds from gillnets. The catch rate of northern pike 21.00 inches and larger was moderate (5.67 fish/gillnet) in the 2004 assessment. Northern pike growth rates for Foot Lake were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-3. The 2001 northern pike year class comprised 44% of the 2004 assessment northern pike catch.

Bluegill numbers were abundant in 2004 (27.67 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The bluegill historical catch rate is 8.70 fish/trapnet for Foot Lake. The 2004 bluegill average size was moderate (0.23 pounds and 6.34 inches) from trapnets. The bluegill historical average weight is 0.29 pounds from trapnets.

Black crappie numbers were abundant in 2004 (8.17 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The black crappie historical catch rate is 5.20 fish/trapnet for Foot Lake. The 2004 black crappie average size was small (0.16 pounds and 6.20 inches) from trapnets. The black crappie historical average weight is 0.35 pounds from trapnets. The 2003 black crappie year class comprised 59% of the 2004 black crappie assessment catch.

Walleye numbers were low in 2004 (0.33 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The Foot Lake walleye historical catch rate is 6.33 fish/gillnet for Foot Lake. The 2004 walleye average size was large (4.28 pounds and 22.68 inches) from gillnets. The walleye historical average weight is 1.65 pounds from gillnets. Walleye growth rates for Foot Lake were above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-4. The 2000 walleye year class comprised 52% of the 2004 walleye assessment catch. Walleye natural reproduction in Foot Lake is generally infrequent and insignificant based on previous fall and summer surveys.

A fall electrofishing survey was conducted to evaluate a 2004 spring walleye fry stocking (494,712 fry). There were low to moderate young of year "YOY" walleye numbers (44.12 YOY/hour, 6.04 inches average length) captured in 2004. Other area walleye fry stocked lakes had generally low YOY walleye catch rates in 2004 fall electrofishing surveys. The Foot Lake fall electrofishing historical average catch rate and length are 158.69 YOY/hour and 5.67 inches respectively. Walleye fry have been stocked recently into Foot during 1994, 1996, 1998, 2000, and 2002. The Foot Lake highest fall electrofishing catch rate of YOY walleye occurred in 1994 (516.00 YOY/hour, 4.80 inches average length) for a fry stocked year.

Black bullhead numbers were abundant in 2004 (124.67 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The black bullhead historical average catch rate is 153.87 fish/gillnet for Foot Lake. The 2004 black bullhead average size was moderate (0.34 pounds and 8.30 inches) from gillnets. The black bullhead historical average weight is 0.26 pounds from gillnets.

Carp numbers were moderate in 2004 (1.33 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The carp historical average catch rate is 3.77 fish/trapnet for Foot Lake. The 2004 carp average size was moderate (5.37 pounds and 21.98 inches) from trapnets. The carp historical average weight is 3.93 pounds from trapnets.

Freshwater drum numbers were low in 2004 (1.50 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The freshwater drum historical average catch rate is 3.27 fish/trapnet for Foot Lake. The 2004 freshwater drum average size was moderate (2.48 pounds and 17.23 inches) from trapnets.

Current fish management activities on Foot include monitoring the fish population on a periodic basis, monitoring winter dissolved oxygen levels, assisting the county with the operation of the aeration system, protecting aquatic vegetation through the permit process, participating in local watershed initiatives, and stocking various fish species as warranted. The Foot fishery will be sampled in the 2008 summer for all fish species.
