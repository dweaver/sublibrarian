* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 07/16/2008)

A resurvey was conducted on Nest Lake during mid-July of 2008. Nest is a large, productive, and moderately deep lake located in Kandiyohi County. The maximum depth is 40 feet. Residential development (192 homes, several common interest properties, and recreational vehicle sites) has occurred along the entire shoreline area with the exception of the northwest portion of the lake. There are approximately 20 inlets/tiles that enter Nest Lake. Agricultural row crop drainage into the lake occurs via several inlets/tiles from various locations throughout the lake. The major inlet is the Middle Fork of the Crow River, which enters along the north shore of Nest Lake. The Middle Fork Crow Reservoir is located about 4 miles upstream of Nest Lake. The outlet drains into the Green Lake via the Old Mill Inn Dam. Public access sites include one state access along the east portion and one along the west portion of the lake. There are three resorts, one motel, and Sanderson Girl Scout Camp located along Nest Lake. Shoreline substrates are varied with boulder, gravel, sand, rubble, and silt. Boulder and rubble are found near the islands and south shore point areas. Silt is most prevalent in the east bays and north inlet bay area. Emergent vegetation (bulrush, cattails, wild rice) is present in the southwest, west and north portions of the lake. White and yellow waterlilies occur frequently in the east bay areas. Submergent vegetation densities (northern milfoil, coontail, flat-stem pondweed, filamentous algae) within the lake were moderate in 2008. Curly-leaf pondweed can become a nuisance for boating and swimming during most years. Curly-leaf pondweed was abundant in the littoral areas along the north, east, and west shore areas during June of 2008. The maximum depth of vegetation was 12.0 feet. Mid summer-dissolved oxygen levels were below 3.0 ppm at depths greater than 21.0 feet. Water quality (total phosphorus=0.028 mg/l) was moderate and clarity (secchi=8.0 feet on 6/09/08, 4.1 feet on 7/16/08) was fair during 2008. Algae blooms can occur during the late summer months. Non-point nutrient input sources into Nest include residential homes, agricultural row crops, road runoff, and city of New London. Nest Lake is managed for walleye, northern pike, largemouth bass, bluegill, and black crappie. Nest Lake is scheduled for walleye fingerling stocking after consecutive poor YOY walleye numbers due to fry stocking or natural reproduction. Nest is managed primarily for walleye.

A spring electrofishing survey was not conducted in Nest during 2008. The previous 2003 spring electrofishing survey results included moderate numbers of largemouth bass (30.46 fish/hour) compared to the Nest Lake historical average (31.75 fish/hour). The 2003 largemouth bass average weight and length were 1.82 pounds and 14.17 inches respectively from spring electrofishing. The largemouth bass historical average weight and length are 1.22 pounds and 12.1 inches from spring electrofishing. There were abundant numbers of young of year "YOY" largemouth bass (18.88 YOY/seine haul, 2.56 inches average length) captured in the 2008 shoreline seine hauls.

Smallmouth bass numbers were low in 2008 (0.20 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The smallmouth bass historical average catch rate is 0.43 fish/gillnet for Nest Lake. The 2008 smallmouth bass average size was large (3.53 pounds and 17.91 inches) from gillnets. The smallmouth bass historical average weight is 1.87 pounds from gillnets. YOY smallmouth bass numbers were low (0.75 YOY/seine haul, 2.19 inches average length) in the 2008 shoreline seine hauls.

Black crappie numbers were moderate to low in 2008 (2.80 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for Nest (25th=1.56, 50th=2.80, 75th=6.54 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The black crappie gillnet historical average catch rate was 7.03 fish/gillnet. The black crappie catch rate from the 2008 trapnets (1.58 fish/trapnet) was near the historical average (1.79 fish/trapnet). The 2008 black crappie average size was moderate (0.29 pounds and 8.07 inches) from gillnets. The 2008 catch rate of quality size (8.00 inches) and larger black crappie was low (1.20 fish/gillnet). Black crappie growth rates were generally below the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-4. The 2005 year class comprised 59% of the 2008 total black crappie resurvey catch. No YOY black crappie were captured in the 2008 shoreline seine hauls.

Northern pike numbers were abundant in 2008 (9.80 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for Nest Lake (25th=2.85, 50th=5.33, 75th=6.29 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The northern pike historical average catch rate is 4.98 fish/gillnet. The 2008 northern pike average size was moderate (3.04 pounds and 23.51 inches) from gillnets. The northern pike historical average weight is 2.82 pounds from gillnets. The 2008 catch rate of quality size (21.00 inches) and larger northern pike was high (6.60 fish/gillnet). The 2008 catch rate of preferred size (28.00 inches) and larger was moderate (1.20 fish/gillnet). Northern pike growth rates were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-7. Approximately 39% of the northern pike captured in the 2008 summer resurvey were from the 2004 year class. Low numbers of YOY northern pike (0.88 YOY/seine haul, 5.56 inches average length) were captured in the 2008 shoreline seine hauls.

Yellow perch numbers were low in 2008 (3.20 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal ranges for Nest Lake (25th=10.38, 50th=15.50, 75th=29.81 fish/gillnet) and similar lakes. The yellow perch historical average catch rate is 18.25 fish/gillnet. The 2008 yellow perch average weight was low (0.08 pounds) from gillnets. The 2008 yellow perch average length (5.77 inches) from gillnets was slightly below the historical average (5.90 inches). Yellow perch growth rates were below the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-3. All of the yellow perch captured from the 2008 gillnets belonged to the 2005 year class. Abundant numbers of YOY yellow perch were captured in the 2008 shoreline seine hauls (31.25 YOY/seine haul, 2.28 inches average length).

Walleye numbers were low in 2008 (2.80 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for Nest Lake (25th=4.75, 50th=6.25, 75th=8.27 fish/gillnet), but within the normal range for similar lakes. The walleye historical catch rate is 6.87 fish/gillnet for Nest Lake. The 2008 walleye average size was moderate to large (3.04 pounds and 19.67 inches) from gillnets. The walleye historical average weight is 1.53 pounds from gillnets. The 2008 catch rate of quality size (15.00 inches) and larger walleye (2.20 fish/gillnet) was equal to the Nest Lake historical average. Walleye growth rates were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-8. A large 2001 walleye year class (from natural reproduction) comprised 65% of the 2003 and 53% of the 2008 total walleye catch from survey nets. Low numbers of walleye were captured from ice out trapnets in 2008 (1.70 fish/trapnet, 2.41 pounds average weight, 18.90 inches average length). All of the walleye captured were males during the ice out trapnetting. Approximately 29% of the walleye captured in the ice out trapnets were from the 2001 year class.

The walleye population is due to a combination of natural reproduction and walleye stocking (fry, fingerlings, adults) in Nest Lake. Nest Lake occasionally receives low numbers of walleye fry via the Middle Fork of the Crow River during clean up operations of the walleye hatching program at the New London State Fish Hatchery. There were low YOY walleye numbers captured (due to natural reproduction or fry stocking) in 2008 (1.50 YOY/hour, 6.61 inches average length). The YOY walleye historical average catch rate is 28.47 YOY/hour for Nest Lake. The Spicer Area YOY walleye average catch rate is 76.32 YOY/hour. Nest Lake was recently stocked in 2004 (10,994 fry, 580 adults, 10,775 fingerlings), 2005 (377,879 fry), 2006 (532,727 fry, 113 adults, 26,766 fingerlings, 16 yearlings), 2007 (51,429 fry), and 2008 (694,029 fry).

Bluegill numbers were moderate in 2008 (37.67 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal ranges for Nest Lake (25th=25.75, 50th=37.67, 75th=47.43 fish/trapnet) and similar lakes. Abundant numbers of bluegill were captured in the 2008 gillnets (28.20 fish/gillnet, 0.23 pounds average weight). The bluegill historical catch rate is 36.25 fish/trapnet. The 2008 bluegill average size was moderate (0.21 pounds and 6.20 inches) from trapnets. The bluegill historical average weight is 0.17 pounds from trapnets. The 2008 catch rate of quality size (6.00 inches) and larger bluegill was high (26.28 fish/trapnet). Bluegill growth rates were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-6, but above the normal ranges for ages 7-8. The 2002 year class comprised 36% of the total bluegill catch in the 2008 gillnets and trapnets. YOY bluegill numbers were low (0.38 YOY/seine haul, 1.17 inches average length) in the 2008 shoreline seine hauls.

Black bullhead numbers were low in 2008 (2.40 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The 2008 black bullhead average size was large (1.18 pounds and 12.50 inches) from gillnets. Yellow bullhead numbers were moderate to abundant in 2008 (6.80 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The 2008 yellow bullhead average size was large (0.82 pounds and 11.16 inches) from gillnets.

Other fish species of special interest captured in the 2008 included moderate numbers of white sucker (3.00 fish/gillnet). The white sucker historical average catch rate is 3.68 fish/gillnet. The 2008 white sucker average size was moderate to large (2.89 pounds and 18.70 inches) from gillnets.

Current fish management activities on Nest Lake include monitoring the fish population on a periodic basis, protecting aquatic vegetation through the permit process, participating in local watershed initiatives, and stocking various fish species as warranted. A fall electrofishing survey for YOY walleye is scheduled for 2009. A population assessment of all fish species is scheduled during the 2013 summer for Nest Lake. ?
