Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Fish Consumption Guidelines

No fish consumption guidelines are available for this lake. For more information, see the "Fish Consumption Advice" pages at the Minnesota Department of Health.


Status of the Fishery (as of 06/20/2005)

A fish resurvey was conducted during mid June of 2005 in Swenson Lake. Swenson is a small sized (109 acres), shallow (14 feet maximum depth), aerated, and productive lake located in Kandiyohi County. Residential development is limited to 18 homes along the north and west shore areas. The public access is located on the north side. The surrounding watershed comprises mostly of agricultural row crops with small areas of woodland and wetland areas. The immediate shoreline area consists mostly of undeveloped woodland (hardwood trees) with small areas of wetlands, row crops, and residential homes. Agricultural row crop runoff (via inlets) is the primary source of nutrient pollution into Swenson. Nutrient levels were high (total phosphorus=0.081 ppm) during 2005 in Swenson. There are three small to moderate sized inlets (north, west, and south shores) that flow into Swenson. The outlet connects to Shakopee Creek. Water clarity was good (secchi= 6.00 feet) during the June 2005 resurvey. However, blue-green algae blooms were common and intense after the survey during the mid summer months in Swenson Lake. Submergent vegetation is limited to primarily sago pondweed, water moss, and filamentous algae, especially in the near shore areas. Emergent vegetation consists mainly of bulrush and cattail species scattered throughout the shoreline areas. Shoalwater substrates consist primarily of sand, gravel, and rubble. Swenson Lake is managed primarily for walleye, northern pike, black crappie, yellow perch, and bluegill. Walleye fry are generally stocked into Swenson every other year. If a fry stocking is a failure, then the lake will generally be stocked again with fry the following spring. Walleye fingerling stocking is contingent upon two successive walleye fry stocking failures in Swenson Lake.

Northern pike numbers were moderate in 2005 (4.33 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The northern pike historical average catch rate is 7.03 fish/gillnet for Swenson. The 2005 northern pike average size was large (6.56 pounds and 30.1 inches) from gillnets. The northern pike historical average weight is 3.15 pounds from gillnets. The 2005 catch rate of preferred size (28.0 inches) and larger northern pike was high (2.67 fish/gillnet) for Swenson. Northern pike growth rates in Swenson were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-8. The 1998 and 1999 northern pike year classes each comprised 22% of the 2005 northern pike resurvey catch. The largest northern pike captured was 37.2 inches in the 2005 resurvey.

Black crappie numbers were abundant in 2005 (21.60 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The black crappie historical average catch rate is 8.88 fish/trapnet for Swenson. The 2005 black crappie average size was moderate (0.38 pounds and 8.6 inches) from trapnets. The black crappie historical average weight is 0.22 pounds from trapnets. The 2005 catch rate of quality size (8.0 inches) and larger black crappie was high (16.90 fish/trapnet) for Swenson. Black crappie growth rates in Swenson were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-5. The 2001 black crappie year class comprised 44% of the 2005 black crappie resurvey catch. The largest black crappie captured was 10.6 inches in the 2005 resurvey.

Yellow perch numbers were moderate in 2005 (11.67 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, but below the historical average catch rate (20.47 fish/gillnet) for Swenson Lake. The 2005 yellow perch average size was moderate (0.18 pounds and 6.9 inches) from gillnets. The yellow perch historical average weight is 0.22 pounds from gillnets. The 2005 catch rate of quality size (8.0 inches) and larger yellow perch was low (1.33 fish/gillnet) for Swenson. Yellow perch growth rates in Swenson were within the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-5. The 2003 year class comprised 70% of the 2005 yellow perch resurvey catch.

Bluegill numbers were moderate in 2005 (6.70 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The bluegill historical catch rate is 8.03 fish/trapnet for Swenson. The 2005 bluegill average size was large (0.36 pounds and 7.1 inches) from trapnets. The bluegill historical average weight is 0.23 pounds from trapnets. The 2005 catch rate of quality size (6.0 inches) and larger bluegill was moderate (5.60 fish/trapnet) in Swenson. Bluegill growth rates in Swenson Lake were above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 2-6. The 2000 bluegill year class comprised 34% of the 2005 bluegill resurvey catch. Adult bluegill (1,272 fish) were stocked in Swenson during 2001. The largest bluegill captured was 9.2 inches in the 2005 resurvey.

Walleye numbers were low in 2005 (0.33 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The walleye historical average catch rate is 10.72 fish/gillnet for Swenson. The 2005 walleye trapnet catch (2.00 fish/trapnet) was similar to the historical average (1.65 fish/trapnet) in Swenson. The 2005 walleye average size was small (0.96 pounds and 12.2 inches) from trapnets. The walleye historical average weight is 1.90 pounds from trapnets. Walleye growth rates for Swenson were generally above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-8. The 2004 walleye year class (fry stocked in the 2004 spring) comprised 62% of the 2005 walleye resurvey catch. Walleye natural reproduction in Swenson is generally infrequent and insignificant based on previous fall and summer surveys. The largest walleye captured was 27.0 inches from the 2005 resurvey.

Largemouth bass numbers were low in the 2005 spring electrofishing survey (15.00 fish/hour) compared to the Spicer Area lakes average (47.20 fish/hour). The largemouth bass historical average catch rate is 16.95 fish/hour for Swenson. The 2005 largemouth bass average size was moderate to large (1.98 pounds and 14.0 inches) from spring electrofishing. The largemouth bass historical average weight is 2.02 pounds from spring electrofishing for Swenson. The 2005 catch rate of preferred size (15.0 inches) and larger largemouth bass (5.25 fish/hour) was below the Swenson historical average (7.35 fish/hour). Largemouth bass growth rates in Swenson Lake were above the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-8. The 2000 and 2003 year classes each comprised 30% of the 2005 total largemouth bass spring electrofishing catch. The largest largemouth bass captured was 20.2 inches from the 2005 spring electrofishing survey.

Black bullhead numbers were moderate in 2005 (50.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The black bullhead historical average catch rate is 118.03 fish/gillnet for Swenson. The 2005 black bullhead trapnet catch rate (11.60 fish/trapnet) was also below the historical average (159.37 fish/trapnet) for Swenson. The 2005 black bullhead average size was moderate (0.55 pounds and 9.5 inches) from gillnets. The black bullhead historical average weight is 0.34 pounds from gillnets.

Carp numbers were moderate in 2005 (2.10 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes. The carp historical average catch rate is 4.33 fish/trapnet for Swenson Lake. The 2005 carp average size was moderate (5.43 pounds and 21.73 inches) from trapnets. The carp historical average weight is 3.77 pounds from gillnets.

Current fish management activities on Swenson include monitoring the fish population on a periodic basis, monitoring winter dissolved oxygen levels, protecting aquatic vegetation through the permit process, participating in local watershed initiatives, and stocking various fish species as warranted. The Swenson fishery will be sampled in the 2010 summer for all fish species.
