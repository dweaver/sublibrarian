Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 07/09/2007)

A resurvey was conducted at Clear Lake in 2007. Clear has a history of taking partial winterkills, with the latest event occurring during the 2000-2001 winter. Clear is managed for northern pike, black crappie, and walleye.

The lakes watershed was dominated by row-crop agriculture and much of the shoreline was developed with residential homes. A total of 21 aquatic plants were sampled in late July and all were considered rare, except cattail and filamentous algae, which were ranked as common. Curly-leaf pondweed was abundant around much of the lake in the spring. Silt was the only substrate classified as abundant, while detritus and sand were considered common. Gravel and rubble were both classified as rare shoalwater substrates. Shoreland property owners are encouraged to protect emergent aquatic plants. Emergents provide food and cover for many fish and wildlife species, improve water clarity, reduce erosion, and improve aesthetics.

A total of 20 walleye were gill netted for a catch rate of 2.2/net, which indicated that numbers were fair to low for this type of lake. The catch rate in 2003 was 0.9/net. In 2007, gill netted walleye were 11.1-22.1 inches long averaging 18.9 inches. Walleye were 1-6 years old with 6 year-classes present. The most recent stocking regime (3lb/littoral acre every-other-year; initiated in 2005) produced only 1 fish in the 2007 gill netting. A total of 8 fish corresponded with the 2003 stocking regime (1.5lb/littoral acre). Growth was fast.

A total of 23 northern pike were gill netted for a catch rate of 2.6/net, which indicated that fair numbers were present. The northern pike gill net catch rate has not been this low since the 1993 assessment. The catch rate in 2003 was 9.2/net. In 2007, gill netted northern pike were 22.1-32.7 inches long averaging 25.8 inches. Trap nets sampled northern pike up to 36.5 inches in length. Northern pike were 2-8 years old with 6 year-classes present and growth was good. This years decrease in northern pike may allow for better survival of stocked walleye fingerlings.

A total of 14 yellow perch were gill netted for a catch rate of 1.6/net, which indicated that numbers were low. The 2003 catch rate was similar at 2.4/net. In 2007, gill netted yellow perch were 5.7-7.8 inches long. Yellow perch were 2-5 years with 4 year-classes present.

A total of 109 black crappie were trap netted for a catch rate of 12.1/net, which indicated that moderate numbers were present. The 2003 catch rate was similar at 9.8/net. In 2007, trap netted black crappie were 5.4-9.7 inches long averaging 7.1 inches. Gill nets sampled 598 black crappie for a catch rate of 66.4/net, which indicated that numbers were high. Black crappie were 2-6 years old with 5 year-classes present. Age analysis indicated that the 2005 year-class was quite strong, accounting for 94% of the gill net catch. Growth was slow. Assessments in 2003, 2000, and 1997 also showed few fish over 8 inches and slow growth, indicating a stunted population.

A total of 1,019 bluegill were trap netted for a catch rate of 113.2/net, which indicated that numbers were high. In 2003, the catch rate was much lower at 10.7/net. In 2007, trap netted bluegill were 3.4-9.1 inches long averaging 4.9 inches. Approximately 5% of the trap net catch was 7" or longer. Bluegill were 2-7 years old with 6 year-classes present. Age 2 fish accounted for 80% of the trap net catch.

A total of 4 white crappie were trap netted for a catch rate of 0.4/net, which indicated that low numbers were present. Trap netted white crappie were 14.3-15.5 inches long averaging 14.8 inches. The trap netted fish were 6-7 years old with 2 year-classes present. Growth was good.

A total of 552 black bullhead were trap netted for a catch rate of 61.3/net, which indicated that high numbers were present. The catch rate was similar in 2003 at 64.8/net. Trap netted black bullhead were 6.9-8.9 inches long.

A total of 4 largemouth bass were collected during spring night-time electrofishing for a catch rate of 2.7/hour. Only 1 largemouth bass was sampled in similar assessments that were conducted in 2003 and 2005. In 2007, largemouth bass were 6.9-16.1 inches long. Largemouth bass were 2-5 years old with 2 year-classes present. Age 2 fish accounted for 75% of the electrofishing sample.

Shoreline seining sampled young-of-the-year; black bullhead (3), black crappie (2), bluegill (256), hybrid sunfish (43), largemouth bass (1), pumpkinseed sunfish (88) and yellow perch (4). Common shiner, golden shiner, and fathead minnow were also collected. ?
