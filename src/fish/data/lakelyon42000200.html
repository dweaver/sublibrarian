* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Fish Consumption Guidelines

No fish consumption guidelines are available for this lake. For more information, see the "Fish Consumption Advice" pages at the Minnesota Department of Health.


Status of the Fishery (as of 06/25/2007)

School Grove is a small (318 acres), shallow (11 feet maximum), aerated, and productive lake located in Lyon County. A population assessment of School Grove was conducted during late June of 2007 for the fish population. Water quality and clarity conditions are often poor in School Grove due to agricultural runoff. Intense blue-green algal blooms are common during the mid summer. Water clarity was poor (1.8 feet) on June 29, 2007. School Grove is a popular local lake for shore fishing and boat fishing of various species (walleye, black crappie, channel catfish, black bullhead). Channel catfish fingerlings/yearlings have been stocked periodically since 1986. The most recent channel catfish stocking occurred in 2006 (3,213 fingerlings, 17 pounds). A fall electrofishing survey was also conducted to evaluate a 2007 spring walleye fry stocking (245,453 fry). Walleye fry are stocked into School Grove every other year. If a fry stocking is a failure, then the lake will generally be stocked again with fry the following spring. Walleye fingerling stocking is contingent in School Grove after successive fry stocking failures. School Grove has a history of commercial fish removal of black bullhead in the past. School Grove is managed primarily for walleye, channel catfish, and black crappie.

Black crappie numbers were high in the 2007 trapnets (70.89 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, and above the School Grove historical average (19.03 fish/trapnet). The 2007 black crappie average size was moderate to large (0.42 pounds and 9.16 inches) from trapnets. The black crappie historical average length is 8.02 inches from trapnets. There were abundant numbers of quality size (8.00 inches) and larger black crappie (67.11 fish/trapnet) in the 2007 trapnets. Black crappie growth rates were below the Spicer Area trapnets for ages 3-6, but above the normal ranges for ages 1-2. The 2002 and 2001 year classes comprised 55% and 29% respectively of the 2007 total black crappie gillnet and trapnet catch.

Channel catfish numbers were high in 2007 (6.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, and above the School Grove historical average (2.05 fish/gillnet). The 2007 channel catfish average size was moderate (1.82 pounds and 15.73 inches) from gillnets. The channel catfish historical average length is 18.10 inches from gillnets. The largest channel catfish captured in the 2007 assessment was 25.5 inches.

Black bullhead numbers were low in 2007 (18.50 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, and below the School Grove historical average (174.69 fish/gillnet). The 2007 black bullhead average size was small to moderate (0.20 pounds and 7.24 inches) from gillnets. The black bullhead historical average length is 7.45 inches from gillnets.

Carp numbers were abundant in 2007 (47.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, and above the School Grove historical average (12.43 fish/gillnet). The 2007 carp average size was small (1.54 pounds and 14.80 inches) from gillnets. The carp historical average length is 11.22 inches from gillnets.

Walleye numbers were low in the 2007 gillnets (1.00 fish/gillnet) compared to the normal range for similar lakes, and below the School Grove historical average (15.81 fish/gillnet). However, walleye numbers were moderate to high in the 2007 trapnets (4.89 fish/trapnet) compared to the normal ranges for similar lakes, but comparable to the School Grove historical average (5.25 fish/trapnet). The 2007 walleye average size was small to moderate (1.04 pounds and 13.38 inches) from trapnets. The walleye average length is 17.16 inches from trapnets. Walleye growth rates were below the Spicer Area normal ranges for ages 1-5. The 2004 year class comprised 67% of the 2007 total walleye gillnet and trapnet catch. The 2005 and 2002 year classes comprised 16% and 18% respectively of the total walleye gillnet and trapnet catch.

Low numbers of small young of year "YOY" walleye (8.00 YOY/hour, 4.65 inches average length) were present in the 2007 fall electrofishing survey. There were abundant numbers of YOY walleye (72.00 YOY/hour, 5.52 inches average length) captured in the 2005 fall electrofishing survey. The 2004 electrofishing survey results were 12.00 YOY/hour and 3.94 inches average length. The fall electrofishing historical average catch rate and length are 30.38 YOY/hour and 6.01 inches respectively for School Grove. The Spicer Area YOY walleye historical average catch rate is 71.24 YOY/hour. Walleye fry (approximately 200,000 to 300,000 each year) have been stocked into School Grove during 1996, 1998, 2000, 2002, 2004, 2005, and 2007. The highest fall electrofishing catch rate of YOY walleye for School Grove occurred in the 2005 fry stocked year.

Current fish management activities on School Grove include monitoring the fish population on a periodic basis, monitoring winter dissolved oxygen levels, assisting the sportsman's club with the operation of the aeration system, protecting aquatic vegetation through the permit process, participating in local watershed initiatives, and stocking various fish species as warranted. The School Grove fishery will be sampled in the 2008 fall for YOY walleye. ?
