* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 06/23/2008)

Eighth Crow Wing Lake is located three miles south of Nevis in Hubbard County. Eighth Crow Wing has a surface area of 492 acres and a maximum depth of 30 feet. A public access is located on the northeast corner of the lake and boating access is also available from the channel (Crow Wing River) connecting to Ninth Crow Wing then Tenth Crow Wing Lake. There is a small dam at the outlet of Eighth Crow Wing, which restricts boating to Seventh Crow Wing Lake. Eighth Crow Wing Lake provides a diverse fishery to the angler and is well known for abundant walleye and panfish populations as well as large northern pike. A special 40-inch minimum length limit on northern pike was implemented in the spring of 2003.

The Minnesota Department of Natural Resources (DNR) has classified Minnesota's lakes into 43 different types based on physical, chemical, and other characteristics. Eighth Crow Wing is in lake class 31. Other area lakes in this same classification include: Second, Fifth, Sixth, and Seventh Crow Wing Lakes, Boulder, Duck, and Lower Twin.

Eighth Crow Wing has a history of having a low to moderate northern pike population composed of quality-sized fish. Northern pike abundance (5.8 pike/gillnet) was up from past surveys and within the range "typical" for this lake class. Past survey has shown northern pike abundance to be lower, below or at the low end of the range "typical" for this lake class. Sampled northern pike were large-sized with an average length and weight of 30.0 inches and 6.3 pounds. Northern pike up to 37.1 inches were sampled. Northern pike growth rates were very fast when compared to other area lakes. An abundant forage base of tullibee (cisco), white sucker, and yellow perch contribute to the production of large northern pike in Eighth Crow Wing Lake. Future surveys are planned to evaluate the success and effectiveness of the 40-inch minimum size length limit on northern pike.

Walleye abundance (7.3 walleye/gillnet) was at the current management goal of 7.0 walleye/gillnet and above the range "typical" for this lake class. Surveys since 1988 have had similar walleye abundance, at or above the current management goal. Sampled walleye had an average size of 19.6 inches and 2.8 pounds, with fish measured up to 28.6 inches. Walleye fingerling stocking by the DNR is maintaining the abundant walleye numbers in Eighth Crow Wing. Eighth Crow Wing is stocked with walleye fingerlings during odd years.

Eighth Crow Wing supports an abundant largemouth bass and panfish population and can provide good fishing for bluegill, pumpkinseed, black crappie, and largemouth bass. Bluegill were sampled in high numbers (138 bluegill/trapnet), similar to past surveys. Anglers will find good numbers of bluegill and pumpkinseed in the 6-7 inch size range. Black crappie abundance (3.5 crappie/gillnet) was up, and at the upper end of the range "typical" for this lake class. Past surveys have shown Eighth Crow Wing to have a low to moderate black crappie population. Spring electrofishing was conducted for the first time on Eighth Crow Wing and largemouth bass were sampled in high numbers (116 bass/hour) when compared to other area lakes. Anglers will find good numbers of bass in the 14-16 inch size range. Eighth Crow Wing has good habitat of aquatic vegetation cover that will insure a healthy panfish and bass population.

Other species sampled include high numbers of rock bass, and moderate numbers of yellow bullhead, bowfin, hybrid sunfish, and golden shiner. ?
