Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 08/13/2007)

Belle Taine Lake is located in south-central Hubbard County adjacent to the town of Nevis. Belle Taine has a surface area of 1,185 acres and a maximum depth of 56 feet. The public access is located on the east end of the lake and is owned and maintained by the city of Nevis. Access is also available by small craft during "normal" water years from Shallow Lake, which lies to the north of Belle Taine. Water levels in Bell Taine were very high in the late 1990's, 2 to 3 feet above existing high water marks, but have receded the past few years. Belle Taine is a very popular lake for fishing and water recreation. Belle Taine provides good angling opportunities for walleye, northern pike, largemouth bass, smallmouth bass, and black crappie.

The Minnesota Dept. of Natural Resources (DNR) has classified Minnesota's lakes into 43 different types based on physical, chemical, and other characteristics. Belle Taine is in lake class 25. Other area lakes in this same classification include: Bad Axe, West Crooked, Ojibway, Eagle, Island, Big Mantrap, and Little Sand.

Walleye abundance (7.5 walleye/gillnet) was at the current management goal of 7 walleye/gillnet. Surveys since 1991 have had similar walleye abundance, with gillnet catch rates fluctuating just below the management goal. Walleye abundance in earlier surveys has fluctuated from high numbers in the 1980's to low numbers in 1957 and 1973. Walleye gillnet catch rates have ranged from 2.3 walleye/net in 1957 to 29.0/net in 1981. Sampled walleye had an average length and weight of 15.7 inches and 1.3 pounds, with fish measured up to 26.5 inches. Belle Taine is currently stocked with walleye fingerlings during even numbered years. The walleye forage base is a concern in Belle Taine as the abundance of yellow perch is very low. Yellow perch abundance (0.1 perch/gillnet) is the lowest recorded for all surveys and well below the range "typical" for this lake class. Yellow perch numbers have been low since 1986, while yellow perch abundance from earlier surveys were within or above the range "typical" for this lake class.

Northern pike abundance (7.0 pike/gillnet) was up from past surveys, but still within the range "typical" for this lake class. Small northern pike dominated the sample, with an average length and weight of 21.1 inches and 2.0 lbs, and pike measured up to 31.1 inches. Silver pike, a color phase of northern pike, are present in Belle Taine and occasionally caught by anglers.

Belle Taine supports an abundant largemouth bass population as well as moderate numbers of smallmouth bass. Spring electrofishing surveys sampled good numbers of largemouth bass (63 bass/hour) and smallmouth bass (14 bass/hour). Largemouth up to 19.2 inches and smallmouth up to 18.9 inches were sampled. A special tagging study of bass in conjunction with an angler creel survey was conducted in 2007. Results of the tagging and angler creel survey will be used to better manage the smallmouth and largemouth bass populations in Belle Taine. Belle Taine has good water quality, bottom substrate, and submerged and emergent aquatic vegetation that will insure a healthy largemouth and smallmouth bass population.

Good numbers of black crappie are found in Belle Taine and angling for them is popular year around. Black Crappie were sampled in moderate to abundant numbers for all gear types. Anglers will find good numbers of crappie in the 9-10 inch size range. Belle Taine also supports good numbers of bluegill and pumpkinseed, however, size of sunfish runs small. Anglers will find lots of sunfish in the 6.0 size range. Bluegill growth rates were slow when compared to other area lakes.

Other species sampled included high numbers of yellow bullhead, and moderate numbers of white sucker and rock bass.
