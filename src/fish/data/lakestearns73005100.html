* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Fish Consumption Guidelines

No fish consumption guidelines are available for this lake. For more information, see the "Fish Consumption Advice" pages at the Minnesota Department of Health.


Status of the Fishery (as of 08/15/2005)

Pleasant Lake is a small (222 acres) lake located three miles southwest of St. Cloud. It has had experimental regulations restricting the harvest of sunfish to 10/day since 1996. A survey was conducted as part of the evaluation of the regulations, due for review in 2006. Test netting and electrofishing revealed that principal species are northern pike, largemouth bass, and bluegill.

Northern Pike Small northern pike are abundant and should provide good angling. A total of 65 northern pike were sampled and fish ranged in length from 10.7 to 34.4 inches with an average length of 17.4 inches. The average weight of northern pike caught was 1.3 pounds. Anglers are encouraged to harvest small northern pike in order to improve the size structure of the population.

Walleye Walleye abundance has always been low despite the stocking of fingerlings every other year. Pleasant Lake is the type of lake that will provide "incidental" walleyes in the catch as anglers seek other species. Walleye sampled ranged in length from 16.3 to 25.3 inches. The average length and weight of walleye sampled was 21.2 inches and 3.3 pounds.

Bluegill Historically, small bluegill have always been abundant. One reason the experimental fishing regulations haven't seemed to work is because growth rates are slow. Most bluegill ranged in length from 4.5 to 7.0 inches; the largest was 7.2 inches.

Black Crappie With its good water clarity black crappie will be present at lower levels of abundance. Occasional year classes will provide good angling at times. A total of seven black crappies were sampled during the trap net survey. Black crappie ranged in length from 7.2 to 10.9 inches with a mean length of 8.5 inches.

Largemouth Bass Anglers can expect good largemouth bass fishing. The electrofishing catch rate was greater than the mean for Montrose area lakes (52/hr). Mean length of largemouth bass sampled was 11.6 inches, while the largest bass sampled was 19.8 inches and twelve years old.
