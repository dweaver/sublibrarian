Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Fish Consumption Guidelines

No fish consumption guidelines are available for this lake. For more information, see the "Fish Consumption Advice" pages at the Minnesota Department of Health.


Status of the Fishery (as of 06/28/2004)

Clear Lake is a 220 acre lake located 5 miles east of Jenkins in northern Crow Wing County. A public access is located on the north side of the lake. It has a maximum depth of 63 feet and 27% of the lake is 15 feet or less. The lake is moderately developed with 62 homes/cabins along mainly the north and east sides of the lake, most of which are set back off the lake. The south side of the lake is virtually undeveloped with a good deal of emergent vegetation. The aquatic plant community consists of 24 species of which muskgrass and hardstem bulrush are the most common. Aquatic plants are important in providing food, cover, and spawning habitat for fish and other aquatic species. The shallow water substrate is predominately sand and marl.

Walleye have been stocked regularly since 1962 and are currently stocked as fingerlings in alternate years at the rate of 1 pound per littoral acre (59lbs.). Walleye were caught in average numbers in 2004 when compared to similar lakes (3.2/gill net). Average length and weight were 21.5" and 3.7 lbs. The 1996 year class represented 42% of the catch with most of these fish measuring 18.1" to 24.1". Twenty five walleyes were also captured while spring electrofishing for bass. The average length of these fish was 9.5" and 80% were from the 2002 year class. Stocking could account for all but 2 fish. Growth of all fish was normal and 52% were 12" or larger.

Northern pike were caught in above average numbers when compared to similar lakes (15.0/gill net). The 2000 and 2001 year classes represented 57% of the catch with most of these fish measuring 14.4" to 25.7". Average length and weight were 20.0" and 1.7 lbs. These fish had average growth with only 9% measuring 24" or larger.

Spring electrofishing resulted in a largemouth bass catch rate of 129.0/hr. The average length of these fish was 10.2" and 21% were 12" or larger. Growth was slow. One smallmouth bass was also captured during spring electrofishing measuring 12.9".

The bluegill catch rate was the highest ever at 67.3/trap net. Average length of these fish was 5.3" with only 8% measuring 7" or larger. Growth was slow when compared to other Minnesota lakes.

Yellow perch were captured in average numbers at 3.0/gill net. The average length was 6.4" with none larger than 8". The 1998 year class represented 78% of the catch. Growth was slow.

Other fish species captured included black crappie, bowfin (dogfish), green sunfish, hybrid sunfish, pumpkinseed sunfish, rock bass, tullibee (cisco), white sucker, and yellow bullhead.
