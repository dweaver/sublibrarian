Fish Stocking Activity

Privately Stocked Fish
* indicates privately stocked fish. Private stocking includes fish purchased by the DNR for stocking and fish purchased and stocked by private citizens and sporting groups.

Stocking Fish Sizes
Fry - Newly hatched fish that are ready to be stocked usually called "swim-ups". Walleye fry are 1/3 of an inch or around 8 mm.
Fingerling - Fingerlings are one to six months old and can range from a size of one to twelve inches depending on the species. Walleye fingerlings range from three to eight inches each fall.
Yearling - Yearling fish are at least one year old. A one-year-old fish can range from three to twenty inches depending on the species. Walleye yearlings average from six to twelve inches.
Adult - Adult fish are fish that have reached maturity. Depending on the species, maturity can be reached at two years of age. Walleye reach maturity between the ages of four and six years.

Status of the Fishery (as of 07/30/2007)

A resurvey was conducted on Betty/Betsy Lake in 2007. Betty is primarily managed for northern pike and black crappie.

The lake's watershed was dominated by agriculture. Sediments and nutrients were both negatively affecting water quality at Betty Lake. Bank erosion was noted, on the Clearwater River, between the public access and Betty Lake. Much of the shoreline remains undeveloped with approximately 1/3 of it being residential homes. Only12 aquatic plant species were sampled and all were classified as being rare. The secchi disk reading in late July was just 1 foot, due to an intense algal bloom. Silt was abundant, sand and marl were common, while detritus and rubble were ranked as rare shoalwater substrates. A drought occurred in the summer 2007 and the water level was low. Dissolved oxygen concentrations below 7 feet were low by late July, thus reducing available fish habitat. Shoreland owners are encouraged to protect emergent aquatic plants. Cattails provide food and cover for many fish and wildlife species, improve water clarity, reduce erosion, and improve aesthetics.

A total of 10 walleye were gill netted for a catch rate of 1.7/net, indicating a small population to be present. The catch rate in 2002 was 3.8/net. In 2007, walleye were 15.7-26.5 inches long averaging 21.9 inches. A 28.3 inch walleye was captured in a trap net. Walleye were 2-10 years old with 7 year-classes present. The current population was self-sustaining, and walleye were last stocked in 1992.

A total of 63 northern pike were gill netted for a catch rate of 10.5/net, indicating a large population. The catch rate in 2002 was 6.8/net. Catch rates have been increasing since the 1997 netting. In 2007, northern pike were 13.9-27.9 inches long averaging 21.9 inches. Northern pike were 1-5 years old with 5 year-classes present. Age 3 fish accounted for 43% of the gill net sample.

A total of 2 yellow perch were gill netted for a catch rate of 0.3/net The catch rate in 2002 was 7.5/net. In 2007, gill netted yellow perch were 5.4-7.4 inches long averaging 6.4 inches. Trap nets sampled yellow perch up to 8.7 inches in length. Yellow perch were 1-5 years old with 3 year-classes present.

A total of 32 channel catfish were gill netted for a catch rate of 5.3/net, indicating a moderate population. The catch rate in 2002 was similar at 5.2/net. Gill net catch rates have been increasing since the 1986 assessment. In 2007, channel catfish were 7.7-24.7 inches long averaging 13.5 inches. Trap nets sampled few (7) channel catfish, but maximum length was slightly higher at 26.3 inches. Approximately 66% of the gill net sample was less than 11 inches in length. A pectoral spine was removed for age estimation. Channel catfish were 2-15 years old with 10 year-classes present. Approximately 53% of those fish were age 2. A one time stocking of channel catfish occurred in 1983, thus indicating that a self sustaining population was present at this time.

A total of 90 black crappie were trap netted for a catch rate of 10.0/net. The catch rate in 2002 was 19.3/net. In 2007, trap netted black crappie were 4.4-11.6 inches long averaging 8.3 inches. Approximately 23% of the trap net sample was 9 inches or longer. Gill netted black crappie were 4.3-11.8 inches long. Black crappie were 1-6 years old with 6 year-classes present. Age 2 fish accounted for 58% of the trap net catch and 55% of the gill net catch indicating a stronger year-class. Gill and trap net catches indicated a moderate population to be present.

A total of 22 bluegill were trap netted for a catch rate of 2.4/net, indicating that the population size was small. The catch rate in 2002 was 13.9/net. In 2007, bluegill were 3.3-6.6 inches long averaging 5.6 inches. Bluegill were 1-4 years old with 4 year-classes present. Poor water quality and the lack of submergent vegetation makes Betty a marginal bluegill lake most years.

A total of 4 black bullhead were trap netted for a catch rate of 0.4/net. The catch rate in 2002 was 4.1/net. In 2007, black bullhead were 9.9-10.8 inches long averaging 10.4 inches. The gill net catch rate was 33.7/net with fish averaging 10.0 inches in length.

A total of 8 carp were trap netted for a catch rate of 0.9/net. The catch rate in 2002 was 1.0/net. In 2007, carp were 24.1-30.4 inches long averaging 26.5 inches. Gill nets also sampled low numbers (3) of carp in 2007.

A total of 12 largemouth bass were collected during spring night-time electrofishing, for a catch rate of 10.5/hour. A similar assessment conducted in 1997 failed to sample any largemouth bass. In 2007, electrofished largemouth bass were 8.5-16.2 inches long averaging 11.4 inches. Trap nets sampled 5 largemouth bass ranging in length from 12.3-17.4 inches and averaging 15.0 inches. Largemouth bass were 2-5 years old with 4 year-classes present. Poor water quality and the lack of submergent vegetation make Betty a marginal largemouth bass lake most years.

A total of 9 different varieties of fish were sampled during shoreline seining. Young-of-the-year were collected for black crappie (19), bluegill (127), largemouth bass (37), northern pike (2), and yellow perch (11). ?
